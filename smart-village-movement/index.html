What we would handle for Smart Village Movement

Built around what you described on our call: pledges spread over three or more years, a cardiac and vascular hospital about to break ground, weekly wires to India, and a board that needs to see it clearly.

01

Monthly bookkeeping and close

Full reconciliation of bank accounts, credit cards, and QuickBooks every month. You receive a Statement of Activity and a Statement of Financial Position on a fixed calendar, not whenever it happens to be finished.

Core
02

Restricted fund tracking

Every incoming dollar tagged to the program it belongs to. At any moment you can pull a report showing what a fund has received, what it has spent, and exactly what remains. This is the piece that turns a fundraiser into something you can steward and defend.

Core
03

Cash flow projection

The problem you named first. Pledges arriving across three or more years, modeled against when construction costs actually hit, so the board can see the gap before it becomes a gap.

Core
04

04 — To begin

One step starts it

Add us to your QuickBooks as an accountant user. That is the whole ask. The assessment is free, and there is no obligation attached to it.

Invite us into QuickBooks Online

Whoever holds admin access on the QuickBooks file, likely Rebecca, can send the invite in about a minute.

Sign in to QuickBooks Online → Settings ⚙ → Manage users → Accountants → Invite
Invite this address accounting@turnkeycfo.com

QuickBooks emails us the invitation and we accept it. Nothing else is needed from your team.

We run the free assessment

We review the file as it stands today: how the chart of accounts is built, whether restricted funds are actually traceable, how the India wires are being recorded, and what the 990 would need that is not there yet. We report what we find plainly, including anything that is already in good shape.

You receive a dashboard built on your real numbers

The same dashboard shown in the previous tab, built from Smart Village Movement's actual QuickBooks data, so the board is looking at itself rather than at an example.

Tiered options for the board

A clear set of options, from core bookkeeping through the added lanes, priced against what the assessment actually finds. The board decides from there. If the answer is no, you keep the assessment and the dashboard regardless.

Read only to start

The assessment is a review. We change nothing in your file without asking first.

Your CPA stays

We work alongside the CPA who files your 990. We are not replacing that relationship.

Payments stay in house

The chairman keeps payment authority. We document and reconcile, we do not take the keys.
