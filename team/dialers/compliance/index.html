Purpose

Every dial a Turnkey Web rep makes is regulated activity. This SOP is the compliance layer under the daily workflow: what happens before a list ever reaches your queue, what you do the moment a prospect says stop, when calling is allowed, how call recording consent is handled, why caller ID is never manipulated, and what the automated SMS system already does on your behalf. You read and acknowledge this SOP before your first live dial, and you re-acknowledge it once a year.

The short version: we scrub before we dial, we honor every stop request the same day and forever, we call only inside the prospect's legal hours, we always say truthfully who we are, and when in doubt we do not make the call.

Cadence and triggers

  • Pre-dial scrub: before any new lead batch or list segment is assigned to a rep. No batch skips it.
  • In-call: the instant a prospect asks not to be called; suppression is logged the same day.
  • Every dialing day: the calling window and identity rules apply to every single call.
  • Onboarding: read this SOP in full and acknowledge in writing before the first live dial.
  • Annually: every rep re-reads and re-acknowledges; Ricky reviews the SOP against current federal and state rules.
  • Same day, ad hoc: any complaint, removal demand, or mention of attorneys or regulators goes to Ricky immediately.

The process

We call businesses, and that still requires care

Turnkey Web dials businesses, nonprofits, and churches, not consumer lists. That business-to-business posture helps, but it does not make the rules optional:

  • Many small-business numbers are the owner's personal cell phone. A sole proprietor's cell is, for practical purposes, a consumer number, and the National Do Not Call Registry can cover it no matter how we sourced it.
  • Several states apply do-not-call and calling-hour rules to business lines too, and state rules can be stricter than federal ones.
  • Call-recording consent laws apply to every call, business or not.

The operating stance is simple: run consumer-grade discipline on every call, and the business-to-business question never has to be litigated.

Pre-dial list hygiene (before a batch is dialed)

No list segment goes live into a rep's campaigns until it has been scrubbed. Reps do not do the scrubbing; reps verify they only ever dial from scrubbed, assigned queues.

In-call do-not-call requests (immediate and permanent)

Calling hours

Call recording consent

  • Every call is recorded through CloudTalk on company systems, and reps consent to that in their agreement. Never record a prospect call on a personal device or any tool outside CloudTalk.
  • On the prospect's side, a number of states require ALL parties to consent to recording (commonly listed: California, Florida, Illinois, Maryland, Massachusetts, Montana, New Hampshire, Pennsylvania, and Washington, among others). Know that these states exist; the exact list shifts, and the firm tracks it centrally.
  • The firm's stance on recording configuration for all-party-consent states (announce recording on every call, disable recording by state, or geo-based handling inside CloudTalk) is set centrally: (confirm with Ricky). Do not improvise your own disclosure script; if you are about to dial into an all-party-consent state and have not been told the current posture, ask first.

Caller ID and identity

SMS follow-up compliance (the system's job, not yours)

All prospect texting is system-sent from the company's registered number. Reps never text prospects from any personal number or device. The controls already built into the system:

  • Baseline-first activation: when the SMS system first went live, existing contacts past the threshold were marked as pre-existing without being texted, so there was never a backlog blast. Any new outbound-touch automation follows the same pattern.
  • One text, ever, per contact, with proper sender identity and opt-out language in the body.
  • Suppression is honored automatically: anyone suppressed, anyone tagged do-not-call or wrong number, and anyone who has ever answered a call is never texted.
  • Opt-outs are permanent. A reply asking to stop texts is honored by the system; your part is to tag Not Interested / Hard No the same day if a prospect tells you on a call to stop texting them.

Annual re-acknowledgment

Guardrails

  • Suppression is permanent, cross-rep, and cross-campaign. No contact ever comes off the suppression list, no matter how much time has passed or which rep is asking.
  • Never dial outside 8am to 9pm in the prospect's local time zone. Stricter state rules override the federal window.
  • Every do-not-call request is tagged and logged the same day. Never batched, never deferred.
  • All recording happens in CloudTalk on company systems. Never on a personal device.
  • Truthful identity on every call. Real name, real company, real caller ID, no exceptions and no clever framing.
  • All prospect SMS is system-sent from company numbers. A rep texting a prospect from a personal phone is a fireable compliance breach, not a style issue.
  • No live dialing without a written compliance acknowledgment on file, and no continued dialing past a lapsed annual re-acknowledgment.
  • When unsure whether a call is compliant, do not make it. Ask first. A skipped dial costs nothing; a bad one is not undoable.
