Purpose

This checklist takes a new Turnkey Web dialing rep from a signed agreement to their first approved solo calling session. Work it top to bottom. Every item is either done or not done, and a rep does not dial a live prospect until every gate before that step is checked off. The goal is a rep who can run their daily campaigns, tag every call correctly, and book meetings without anyone standing over their shoulder.

Owner and roles

Role Who Responsibility in onboarding
Hiring owner Ricky Signs the agreement, approves the batch, sets commission terms, gives final solo sign-off
Systems setup Ricky (or a delegated ops person) CloudTalk account, HubSpot wiring, Slack access, bridge configuration
Training lead Head of Sales (senior rep) or Ricky Script walkthrough, objection training, shadow calls (confirm with Ricky which of the two runs training for a given hire)
New rep The contractor being onboarded Completes paperwork, installs tools, works through training, passes the sign-off call

New reps are 1099 independent contractors of West Turnkey Holdings LLC, contracted under the Turnkey Web brand. They are not employees.

Cadence and triggers

  • Trigger: a candidate has accepted the offer and is ready to sign. Do not begin systems setup before the agreement is signed.
  • Target timeline: day one covers paperwork and account setup; training and shadowing typically span the first several working days; the first solo session follows sign-off (confirm exact day targets with Ricky, they flex with the hire).
  • One-time per rep. Re-run the relevant sections only if a rep returns after an extended gap or moves to a new batch under a renewal.

The process

1. Agreement and paperwork (gate: nothing else starts until this is done)

2. CloudTalk account and number assignment

Each rep gets a fully separate CloudTalk account. Nothing is shared between reps: not the login, not the bridge secret, not the campaigns.

3. HubSpot access wiring

HubSpot is the brain of the dialing system. Reps never manually edit HubSpot records; the tag they apply in CloudTalk drives everything downstream through the bridge.

4. Slack and calendar access

5. Lead list assignment

6. Script and objection training

7. Shadow calls

8. Gates before live dialing

Live dialing stays blocked until all five of these artifacts exist and have been verified. The training lead checks them; Ricky's sign-off (next step) confirms them.

Fail a gate

A gate that is not passed keeps dialing blocked. There is no partial credit and no dialing "while we sort it out."

  • Dialing stays blocked until the specific gate passes. The training lead follows up on training gates (compliance acknowledgment, shadow and supervised calls); Ricky follows up on paperwork and systems gates (agreement, CloudTalk, HubSpot).
  • Retry window: the rep gets a focused retry within one working week of the failed gate, with the gap named plainly (for example: re-drill the two most-missed tags, then redo one supervised session).
  • Two failed retries on the same gate triggers a fit conversation with Ricky: either one defined final retry with a specific bar, or the engagement ends before live dialing begins. Ending at this stage is a clean, no-fault exit; the rep is paid anything owed under their agreement and no batch is assigned.
  • A systems-gate failure (CloudTalk or HubSpot wiring that will not verify) is not the rep's failure. It pauses the rep's timeline and goes to Ricky to fix; the retry window starts once the wiring works.

9. First solo session and sign-off

10. Payment setup (method only)

Guardrails

  • No live dialing before all five gates in step 8 are passed (signed agreement, provisioned CloudTalk account, verified HubSpot access, written compliance acknowledgment, 3 shadow plus 3 supervised calls) AND Ricky's explicit solo sign-off.
  • The 9 tag names, the three-campaign structure, and the follow-up intervals are locked. Never change them without coordinating with Ricky.
  • Reps never manually edit HubSpot contacts. The CloudTalk tag is the only input; the bridge does the rest.
  • Nothing goes to a prospect (follow-up email, proposal, preview link) without the named "APPROVED" reply in the sales channel first.
  • CloudTalk accounts, bridge routes, and secrets are strictly per rep. Never share a login or reuse a secret.
  • All invites and prospect emails send from the shared bookings account or a company role address, never a personal address.
  • Confidential materials (scripts, lead lists, recordings, credentials) stay inside company systems. No exceptions.
  • Every do-not-call request is honored immediately and permanently. Compliance is not coachable-later; it is day one.
  • Payment method setup only in this SOP. Rates, draws, and commission percentages live in the individual signed agreement, not here.

Escalation

Situation Path
Prospect asks about pricing or scope beyond the standard offer Rep pauses, takes the question, escalates to Ricky in Slack same day
Prospect requests do not call Tag Not Interested / Hard No immediately; no escalation needed, suppression is automatic
Prospect says another Turnkey caller already reached them Tag honestly, flag in the sales channel for dedup review
Tag applied but the contact does not re-queue correctly Report in Slack; ops replays the missed event through the bridge recovery tool
CloudTalk campaign not reflecting a HubSpot change after 15+ minutes Ops unlinks and re-links the list in CloudTalk campaign settings before contacting CloudTalk support
Rep unsure which tag fits a call Ask the training lead in Slack before guessing; a wrong tag pollutes the queue for weeks
Anything touching commission terms, batch scope, or the agreement itself Ricky only, in writing
