Purpose

CloudTalk is the dialer every Turnkey Web sales rep uses to work the daily calling campaigns. This guide covers the hands-on mechanics: logging in, making calls, applying the after-call tag, scheduling callbacks, seeing SMS activity, and understanding what happens automatically after you hang up.

Here is the one idea that makes everything else make sense: CloudTalk is the phone, HubSpot is the brain. When you finish a call and apply a tag, an automation reads that tag, updates the contact in HubSpot, and re-files the contact into the right campaign on the right future day. You never update HubSpot by hand. Your only job after each call is to apply the correct tag, and the system handles the rest.

Owner and roles

Role Who Responsibility
System owner Ricky Owns the tag set, campaign structure, numbers, automations, and all configuration changes
Rep Each dialing rep Makes calls, applies exactly one tag per call, fills the callback field when scheduling, flags issues
Rep account isolation Per rep Every rep has a fully separate CloudTalk account with their own tags, campaigns, and automation. You only ever see and touch your own setup

Each rep's account is wired independently to HubSpot. That isolation is deliberate: it keeps your pipeline yours and makes problems traceable. It also means you must never log into, borrow, or "quickly fix" another rep's account.

Cadence and triggers

  • Work your three campaigns every dialing day, in this order: Warm Callbacks first, Follow-ups Due second, Cold New Prospects last.
  • Warm Callbacks should be empty by end of day. Anything left in that queue at close of business is a flag, tell your manager, those are the highest-intent contacts and they must not go stale.
  • If Warm Callbacks and Follow-ups Due are both empty, spend the rest of the day on cold.
  • Contacts flow back into your campaigns automatically based on the tag you applied and its follow-up interval. There is nothing to refresh manually, but expect a short sync delay (see Guardrails).

After-call tagging (the 9 tags)

Every outbound call ends with you applying exactly ONE of the nine standard tags. The tags live on the contact in CloudTalk: after the call, open the contact and add the tag from the tag list. Spelling matters. The automation matches tag names exactly (it forgives capitalization and stray spaces, nothing else). A misspelled tag is logged as unknown and the contact goes nowhere, which means a lead silently falls out of the system.

Tag Use it when What happens next
No Answer Rang out, no human, no voicemail left Comes back to you in 2 days
VM Left You left a voicemail Comes back in 4 days
Gatekeeper You reached a receptionist or screener, not the decision maker Comes back in 3 days, try a different time of day
Callback Scheduled The prospect asked you to call at a specific time Comes back at exactly that time, in your Warm Callbacks queue
Interested - Booked A meeting is booked Leaves the dialing queues, the meeting flow takes over
Interested - Needs Follow Up Warm conversation, no committed meeting yet Comes back in 5 days (or sooner if you set a callback time)
Has Website Already They already have a site they are happy with Removed from Web dialing, flagged as a possible Turnkey CFO bookkeeping referral
Soft No - Follow Up 30 days "Not right now" but not a hard no Comes back in 30 days
Not Interested / Hard No A firm no, or a wrong number Permanently suppressed, never called again

Tag discipline rules:

Callbacks and scheduling

Callback Scheduled is the one tag with a required extra step:

Order matters. The automation reads the callback field at the moment the tag lands. Tag first with an empty field and the system has no time to schedule against.

When a call turns into a booked meeting, tag it Interested - Booked and follow the sales meeting booking SOP for the calendar invite and the Slack booking ping. The dialer's job ends at the tag; the booking process is its own checklist.

SMS visibility

An automated text follow-up runs alongside your dialing. After a contact has gone unanswered several times, the system may send them a single follow-up SMS from a company number. What you need to know:

  • You do not send these texts and you cannot trigger them. The system decides, at most one text ever per contact.
  • Contacts who ever answered a call, and anyone carrying a do-not-contact style tag, are never texted.
  • If a prospect mentions "the text you sent me," that is this system working. Treat it as a warm signal and carry on with the call.
  • SMS replies and text history are visible on the contact's activity in CloudTalk. Read them before a warm callback so you have full context.
  • Never text prospects from your personal phone. All outbound touches happen through company systems.

What syncs to HubSpot automatically

When you apply a tag, this chain fires without you:

  1. Your CloudTalk account's automation sends the tag event to Turnkey's bridge service.
  2. The bridge updates the contact in HubSpot: last tag, timestamp, which rep called, which queue they belong in, and the next call-due date.
  3. HubSpot's lists re-evaluate and re-file the contact.
  4. Your CloudTalk campaigns re-sync from those HubSpot lists, and the contact reappears in the right campaign on the right day.

Practical implications:

  • Never edit contacts directly in HubSpot. Reps do not need HubSpot access to work the system, and manual edits fight the automation.
  • Interested - Booked is the only tag that advances a contact's lifecycle stage in HubSpot. Everything else just schedules or suppresses.
  • Every tag event is logged to an audit trail, so if something looks lost, it can be traced and replayed. Report it rather than re-tagging repeatedly.

Guardrails

  • List sync lag is normal. CloudTalk refreshes from HubSpot roughly every 5 to 10 minutes. A contact you just tagged will not vanish or reappear instantly. Do not re-tag, do not refresh-spam, do not edit anything. If a contact still has not moved after about 15 minutes, flag it in Slack; the standard fix (unlinking and re-linking the list) is done by Ricky, not by reps.
  • Never change these without Ricky's explicit sign-off: the nine tag names, the three-campaign structure, the follow-up day intervals, phone numbers, the callback custom field, the automation ("TKW Tag Router"), or any campaign settings. These are the locked, load-bearing parts of the machine.
  • What IS yours to flex: the order you work within the recommended campaign sequence, your time of day, and your scripts and openers. Those are rep judgment.
  • Never touch another rep's setup. Accounts, tags, campaigns, and automations are per rep by design.
  • Duplicate contacts happen. The same business can appear under more than one rep because of how leads are sourced. If you discover you are dialing someone another rep already reached, do not delete or reassign anything; flag it in Slack for dedup review (confirm with Ricky who owns that review cadence).
  • Compliance is non-negotiable. Honor every do-not-call request immediately with the Not Interested / Hard No tag. No misleading claims, no impersonation. Calls are recorded; conduct yourself accordingly.
  • Confidentiality. CRM records, recordings, dispositions, scripts, and pricing structures stay in company systems only.
  • Billing prompts, plan warnings, or settings screens you did not expect: screenshot, send to Ricky, touch nothing.
