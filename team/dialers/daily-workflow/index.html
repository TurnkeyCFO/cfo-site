Purpose

This SOP is the working day for a Turnkey Web dialer: where your leads come from, the order you work them, how you log every call, what the system does behind the scenes, and how you hand off a booked appointment. A brand-new rep should be able to run a full day from this page alone.

The job in one sentence: work your three campaigns top to bottom, tag every call with exactly one of the nine dispositions, and book website review meetings that prospects actually attend. Turnkey Web sells website design, builds, and a monthly care plan; your win condition is a showed appointment, and the automation handles everything between your calls.

Owner and roles

Role Person Responsibility
Dialing, tagging, booking You (the rep) Work the queues, apply the right tag on every call, book meetings, post the Slack ping
System design, comp, list changes Ricky Sole approver on anything locked (tag names, campaign structure, follow-up intervals)
Duplicate review and list hygiene Ricky / ops Resolves cross-rep contact collisions; you flag, they fix
Behind-the-scenes routing The CloudTalk bridge (automation) Reads your tag, updates HubSpot, re-queues the contact on the right day in the right campaign

Each rep has a fully separate CloudTalk account and their own set of campaigns. You never work another rep's queue.

Cadence and triggers

  • Dialing happens on normal business days, inside the standard window of 9am to 4pm Central (early morning only when it is the prospect's sole availability, and never weekends).
  • Scheduled callbacks are honored at the exact time the prospect requested. That commitment outranks everything else on your board.
  • The three campaigns are worked in the same order every day (see below). Specific daily dial-volume or talk-time targets live in your Offer Letter (confirm with Ricky).

The process

Where your leads come from

You never touch a spreadsheet. Turnkey's lead engines continuously discover two kinds of prospects: high-rated local service businesses with no website at all, and businesses, nonprofits, and churches with a weak existing website. Those leads flow into HubSpot, and your three CloudTalk campaigns each sync from a HubSpot list built for you. CloudTalk refreshes from HubSpot roughly every 5 to 10 minutes, so a contact you tag now reappears on the right day automatically.

Two things follow from that:

  • Work what CloudTalk serves you, top to bottom. The queue order is the priority order. Do not cherry-pick, skip around, or keep a side list.
  • Never edit HubSpot records manually. The tag you apply in CloudTalk is the only input the system needs. Manual edits break the routing.

For weak-website prospects, the record includes specific problems found on their current site (mobile layout broken, outdated design, and similar). Use those as your conversation starters: you are calling about something real you noticed, not reading a generic pitch. For no-website prospects, the angle is their strong Google rating with no site to catch the customers searching for them. Entity type matters too: a small business wants to grow, a nonprofit wants to serve its mission, a church wants to reach its congregation. Match the framing.

Log every call: the nine dispositions

Every outbound call ends with you applying exactly ONE of these nine tags, spelled exactly as written. A typo is not auto-corrected; it logs as an unknown tag and the contact goes nowhere, which means a lead silently rots. The tag names, intervals, and campaign structure are locked; never invent a tag or change a spelling.

Tag When to use it What the system does
No Answer Rang out, no human, no voicemail left Re-queues in 2 days (cold)
VM Left You left a voicemail Re-queues in 4 days (cold)
Gatekeeper Blocked by a receptionist or screener Re-queues in 3 days; try a different time of day
Callback Scheduled Prospect asked for a specific callback time Resurfaces at the exact requested time (warm)
Interested - Booked Meeting booked on the call Leaves your queues; meeting flow takes over
Interested - Needs Follow Up Warm conversation, no committed meeting yet Resurfaces in 5 days (warm), or sooner if you set a callback time
Has Website Already They have a real website and no redesign interest Disqualified from Web; flagged as a possible Turnkey CFO referral
Soft No - Follow Up 30 days Not now, but not a hard no Re-queues in 30 days (cold)
Not Interested / Hard No Genuine no, OR a wrong number Permanent suppression; never called again

Two tags carry an extra step:

Wrong numbers get Not Interested / Hard No. That is intentional; both outcomes mean never call again.

SMS follow-up: the system texts, you do not

An automated follow-up runs alongside your dialing. When a contact crosses a threshold of unanswered dials (currently the third), the system sends them a single follow-up text, once ever per contact, from the company's registered number, with proper identity and opt-out language. It automatically skips anyone suppressed, anyone tagged do-not-call or wrong number, and anyone who has ever answered a call.

Your part:

  • Never send prospects texts from your personal phone or any personal number. All SMS is system-sent, on company numbers, for compliance reasons (recording, opt-out, and carrier registration all live on the company side).
  • If a prospect replies to a text or references one on a call, treat it as warm context and tag the call normally. The tag drives everything downstream.
  • If a prospect asks to stop texts or calls, tag Not Interested / Hard No so suppression is permanent.

Duplicate contacts across reps

The lead engines occasionally create the same business as separate contacts under different reps. Etiquette:

  • If a prospect says another Turnkey rep already called them, apologize briefly, do not re-pitch, and end the call politely. Tag honestly based on what happened, and flag the contact in Slack the same day so ops can consolidate ownership.
  • Never poach: if a contact is clearly mid-conversation with another rep (callback scheduled, interested, or booked by them), it is theirs. Duplicate-collision reviews and any commission question they raise go to Ricky; do not settle ownership between reps yourselves.
  • Do not delete or merge contacts yourself. Flag and move on; consolidation is handled centrally (confirm with Ricky who currently owns the dedup review cadence).

End-of-day wrap

Guardrails

  • The nine tag names, the three-campaign structure, and the follow-up intervals are locked. Changing any of them requires Ricky. Your scripts, openers, and campaign timing within the day are flexible.
  • One tag per call, spelled exactly. The entire routing machine keys off that string.
  • No manual HubSpot edits, no side lists, no personal spreadsheets. Lead data, call notes, and dispositions are company property and live only in company systems. Nothing gets stored on personal email, cloud, or phone, and no screenshots of lead data.
  • Compliance is not fine print. TCPA, state cold-calling and call-recording rules, and Do Not Call all apply. Every call is recorded through company systems. A do-not-call request means Not Interested / Hard No, immediately and permanently. The full rules live in Outbound Calling Compliance.
  • All invites from the shared bookings account, all texts from company numbers. Nothing prospect-facing ever comes from a personal address or device.
  • No weekend bookings, early mornings only when it is the prospect's only option (book it and flag it internally).
  • Pricing and terms questions beyond the standard offer go to Ricky. You book the review meeting; you do not negotiate scope or custom terms on a dial.
  • Tax or legal questions (from any prospect, on any call): route to "your CPA or attorney." We do not answer those.
