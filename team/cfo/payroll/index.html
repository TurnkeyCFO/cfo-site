Purpose

Payroll is the most time-critical thing Turnkey CFO touches. A late or wrong payroll run damages client trust faster than any bookkeeping miss, and a botched payroll transition produces wrong W-2s that follow the client for a full tax year. This SOP covers how we run and support client payroll on Gusto and other providers: the cadence, who approves what, how a payroll takeover is onboarded safely, how payroll notices in accounting@ get triaged, contractor versus employee handling, and 1099 season basics. Turnkey CFO is a bookkeeping firm. We process, reconcile, and report payroll. We do not give tax or legal advice: those questions route to the client's CPA or attorney.

Owner and roles

Role Responsibility
Ricky Sole decision-maker on payroll scope, pricing, which clients we file for versus self-file, and any takeover commitment or cutover date. Follows up directly with a client when an outgoing payroll vendor is slow.
Accounting Manager Owns the payroll calendar across clients, runs or reviews each payroll cycle, owns the transition checklist during a takeover, and is the client-facing contact for payroll questions once ramped.

Client-designated payroll approver Every client names one person authorized to approve payroll and see sensitive pay data (captured in the onboarding Roles & responsibilities section). We never treat anyone else at the client as an approver.

Cadence and triggers

  • Each client has its own pay schedule. Semi-monthly is the most common, but confirm per client and put every run date on the payroll calendar. Never assume a schedule.
  • Compliance rhythm: quarterly Form 941 filings, annual Form 940 (FUTA), W-2s in January, and 1099-NEC season in January. Know for each client whether Turnkey files or the provider auto-files or the client self-files (confirm the per-client filing map with Ricky).
  • Payroll notices land in accounting@turnkeycfo.com continuously and are triaged as part of the daily mailbox pass (see the email triage SOP).
  • Payroll postings must be verified in QBO before the BD3 checkpoint of the monthly close so the close is never waiting on payroll entries.
  • Trigger events: a new client signs with payroll in scope (run the transition process below), a provider or CPA sends a filing-due or discrepancy notice (run the discrepancy process below), or a client asks to change provider, schedule, or comp structure (route to Ricky before committing).

Contractor versus employee handling

  • Employees run through the payroll provider with withholdings. Contractors are paid outside payroll and receive 1099-NEC forms when they meet the reporting threshold.
  • W-9 on file BEFORE first payment to any contractor. No exceptions: chasing W-9s in January is how 1099 season goes wrong.
  • Owner draws are not payroll. For for-profit clients, confirm the owner draw versus payroll treatment during onboarding and keep it consistent.
  • International contractors may require a tax-exemption form above a payment threshold: confirm the requirement per client and collect the form before payments continue (confirm current threshold guidance with Ricky).
  • Worker classification (is this person a contractor or an employee) is a tax and legal question. We flag it; the client's CPA or attorney answers it.

1099 season basics

Per-client variation awareness

Never assume a client is on Gusto. Check the client dossier first. Known patterns:

Client Payroll setup What to know
Heirloom Church ADP Full approval workflow: review preview, verify wages/hours/taxes, approve, confirm bank withdrawal, confirm QBO posting. Clergy housing allowance applies.
Village of Hope Maui Two-entity PEO pattern (processor and legal employer are different entities on the same invoice) Split each invoice into three QBO lines (gross wages, employer taxes, fee). Enter as an Expense, NOT a Bill, because the processor auto-debits the bank account. Match, never add, the bank feed. Gross wages must tie to the payroll register.
Mojo entities Intuit QuickBooks Payroll plus Gusto Heaviest payroll-notification volume in accounting@. Multiple entities: verify which entity each notice belongs to before filing.
Kingdom City Church Gusto (migrated from a prior provider) Migration-era records live in the client's Payroll Handoff folder. Clergy housing allowance applies.
Northpoint Church Gusto (migrated from a prior provider) Migration documentation is in the client folder. Clergy housing allowance applies.
Epic Life Church Gusto Clergy housing allowance applies; retirement administration runs through a separate church retirement platform.

For any client not listed, the client dossier under the clients section of this portal is the source of truth. If the dossier is silent, ask the Accounting Manager; do not guess.

Guardrails

  • The parallel-run YTD verification on any payroll takeover is never skipped. Wrong W-2s are the one payroll mistake we cannot quietly fix later.
  • Only the client-designated approver (or a documented Turnkey grant from Ricky) approves a payroll run. Never approve because "it looked normal."
  • Tax and legal questions (classification, housing allowance eligibility, filing positions, penalties) route to the client's CPA or attorney. We reconcile numbers; we do not advise on tax.
  • Never describe Turnkey CFO with the banned "fractional" CFO-firm label in any client-facing payroll communication. Our language is bookkeeping, payroll, 1099, AP/AR, monthly close, reporting.
  • PII discipline: I-9s, W-4s, and pay registers move through a secure channel and live in the client's Drive folder only. Never in Slack, never in a checklist app, never in a personal inbox.
  • Client-facing payroll email sends from a role address (accounting@turnkeycfo.com or a named role mailbox), never a personal address.
  • QBO discipline: match rather than add on payroll bank-feed transactions; reconcile to a zero difference monthly; follow the client's documented entry pattern (the two-entity Expense-not-Bill pattern exists for a reason).
  • Commitments on payroll scope, pricing, provider changes, or cutover dates are Ricky's alone. Never promise a client a takeover date or a provider migration without his sign-off.

Escalation

Situation Path
Payroll run cannot be verified or approved on time Accounting Manager immediately; client hears from us BEFORE the run date, never after
Tax agency notice, penalty, or missed-filing language Ricky, same day, with the notice attached
Outgoing provider slow during a transition Ricky, who contacts the client directly
Transition date at risk of slipping past roughly two weeks Ricky, before the date blows, with the specific blocked items
Discrepancy flagged by an outside CPA or filer Reconcile first (GL vs gross-to-net vs W-2), then respond with the root cause; if it becomes tax advice, redirect to the client's CPA or attorney
Client asks to change scope, pricing, provider, or schedule Ricky, always; never negotiate solo
Misc payroll charges that will not tie out after provider follow-up Accounting Manager, then Ricky if unresolved
Missing login or access for a payroll platform Post the gap to Slack the day it is found; never work around it or borrow credentials
