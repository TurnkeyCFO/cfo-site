Purpose

This SOP covers everything between "the prospect said yes" and "their first monthly close runs on our standard rhythm." Onboarding done well means the client never has a dropped month, a duplicated month, or a payroll surprise. Onboarding done poorly haunts the engagement for a year: a close that starts from bad opening balances never recovers.

The goal for every new client: signed engagement letter, full system access, a clean cutover with tied opening balances, roles assigned on both sides, and the first close scheduled on the BD1/BD3/BD6/BD10 calendar.

Cadence and triggers

  • Trigger: a prospect confirms they want to proceed (verbally on a call or by email reply).
  • Engagement letter is drafted the same day or next business day. Momentum matters: the observed pattern is QBO access requested same-call or same-day, scoped options sent within about 24 hours.
  • Weekly onboarding sync: set a recurring weekly 1:1 with the client through the end of the onboarding month (or two), then step the cadence down once the first close ships cleanly.
  • Stall rule: any access or document item still open after about 2 weeks is stalled. Surface it to Ricky so he can nudge directly.
  • Payroll deadline rule: if the client runs payroll, the payroll cutover date is the hardest date in the whole onboarding. Slippage tolerance is roughly two weeks maximum before the transition date is treated as blown and reset.

Guardrails

  • No books-of-record work before a signed engagement letter. Access collection and research can start; posting entries cannot.
  • Never skip cutover and opening balances. Shortcutting this section is how months get dropped or duplicated.
  • Never skip the payroll parallel-run verification. Wrong W-2s are not recoverable with an apology.
  • Turnkey CFO is a bookkeeping firm. Client-facing language is bookkeeping, payroll, 1099, AP/AR, monthly close, reporting. Tax or legal questions go to "your CPA or attorney," always.
  • Client-facing email sends from a role address (for example accounting@turnkeycfo.com), never a personal one. No emojis in client-facing copy.
  • View-only means view-only. State the money-movement boundary to the client in writing when requesting bank access.
  • Never borrow credentials or work around a missing login. Post the gap to Slack the day you find it.
  • The client's own brand belongs on their deliverables. Never swap a church or client palette for Turnkey's on client-facing assets.
  • PII travels only through the named secure channel. No I-9s or pay registers sitting in open email threads or checklists.

Escalation

Situation Action
Anything touching pricing, scope, or engagement terms Ricky, always. Never negotiate or promise scope solo.
Access or document item stalled about 2 weeks Surface to Ricky in a recap so he can nudge the client directly
Payroll handoff at risk of missing the cutover date Flag to Ricky immediately; he contacts the client directly
Outgoing bookkeeper unresponsive or withholding records Escalate to Ricky with specifics; he decides the client-side approach
Tax or legal question from the client Redirect to "your CPA or attorney"; do not interpret
Client frustrated, confused, or going quiet mid-onboarding Flag to Ricky same day with a read on why
Opening balances will not tie to the prior close Stop and escalate before posting anything; do not force the tie
