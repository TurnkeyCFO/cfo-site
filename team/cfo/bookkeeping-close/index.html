Purpose

Every Turnkey CFO client gets their prior month's books closed, delivered, and reviewed on a fixed business-day rhythm. This SOP defines that rhythm (BD1, BD3, BD6, BD10, BD12), who owns each milestone, how transactions get categorized and reconciled, what ships in the delivery package, and how to recover when a close slips. Follow it and a brand-new team member can run a close without asking what happens next.

One rule above all: each close cycle delivers the PRIOR month's books. Meetings happen on real, delivered numbers, never on a work in progress.

Cadence and triggers

  • The cadence counts BUSINESS DAYS of the new month, not calendar days. BD1 is the first business day, BD3 the third, and so on. US business holidays do not count as business days.
  • The five milestones: BD1 Request, BD3 Checkpoint, BD6 Deliver, BD10 Finalize (month locks), BD12 Review meeting (about 2 business days after final delivery).
  • Every active client runs a recurring "Turnkey Monthly Bookkeeping" project in Financial Cents. The project's internal task list is the working checklist; client tasks track document uploads (they complete as a set: all pending or all done, never partial).
  • The Close Command site is the team's shared awareness tool: it shows the next two business days of milestones across every client, with checkboxes that sync team-wide. Check it at the start of each close-week day.
  • An older internal document describes a "Day 5" calendar-day close. That model is retired. The business-day cadence in this SOP is canonical.

The process

BD1: Request

BD3: Checkpoint

Categorization (runs BD1 through BD6)

Three lanes exist. Confirm at onboarding which lane a client is on; never assume automation coverage (confirm with Ricky for any client not explicitly assigned).

  1. Manual (default). Hardik categorizes and memos each transaction in QBO. On suggested transfers and deposits, match rather than add, so duplicates never enter the books.
  2. qbo-classifier (daily pilot). Automated daily QBO classification for a small pilot set of clients. Automated write-back is hard-blocked until that client's closed-books date is configured; if it is unset, everything routes to manual review.
  3. ramp-classifier (daily). Ramp card transactions are classified automatically, approved via a Slack card, then written back by browser automation. It never mutates QBO directly. Onboarding a new client to this lane requires a client-specific credential first.

Whatever the lane, Hardik reviews the result. Automation gets the books most of the way; a human stands behind them.

Reconciliation (BD3 through BD6)

BD6: Deliver

The delivery package has three parts:

  1. Financials for the closed month.
  2. Live dashboard refreshed with the closed numbers.
  3. Open Items list: anything still outstanding, stated plainly.

BD10: Finalize and lock

BD12: Review meeting

Meeting cadence variants

Each client is assigned one cadence type:

Type What it means
Review Standard client review meeting on BD12
Board Review on BD12, plus a board reference date on the 3rd Monday of the month, tracked separately
PastorReview (premium) Senior-pastor review on the 2nd Wednesday of the month. This is an ACCELERATED close: financials must be final before mid-week of week 2, so BD10 lock happens before that date. Board or elder reference stays 3rd Monday. Whether BD6 also moves earlier for premium clients: (confirm with Ricky)
Books-only (async) No standing meeting. Delivery and dashboard only. Applies to books-only clients and Turnkey's own internal books (which run the same cadence, lowest priority)
Build Working-session engagement with a quarterly board pack instead of a standard monthly close

Paused clients return to the BD12 review cadence on reactivation.

Holiday handling

  • US business holidays are non-business days; every BD milestone counts around them automatically.
  • If a 3rd Monday board reference lands on a holiday, the board meets an adjacent day; the calendar flags it.
  • If a shifted milestone lands during a premium client's accelerated window, resolve the squeeze in the client's favor: lock earlier, never later. Exact shift rule for edge cases: (confirm with Ricky).

What breaks a close, and how to recover

The number one cause of a delayed close is late card memos (Ramp and Bill.com charges without a memo by BD3). Coding accuracy depends on memo quality, so missing memos stall categorization, which stalls reconciliation, which threatens BD6.

Recovery playbook:

Guardrails

  • Deliver PRIOR month books only. Never review a month that is not locked.
  • Nothing reopens after BD10. A post-lock discovery becomes a next-cycle adjustment.
  • Never deliver financials you would not stand behind. Materially incomplete means escalate first, ship second.
  • Never send a broken or text-only substitute to a client, especially a lead pastor. If a dashboard is not confirmed live, hold the delivery email and page Ricky. No failure language ever reaches a recipient.
  • Client-facing assets use the client's own brand and palette, never Turnkey's swapped in.
  • Client-facing email always sends from a role mailbox (accounting@turnkeycfo.com or a named @turnkeycfo.com address), never a personal address.
  • Financial Cents Client Chat is client-visible; Team Chat is internal. Never mix the two.
  • Turnkey CFO is a bookkeeping firm. Tax or legal questions from a client route to "your CPA or attorney," never answered in-house.
  • Match rather than add in QBO. Duplicated transactions poison every downstream report.
  • Automated QBO write-back stays hard-blocked for any client whose closed-books date is not configured. Do not work around the gate.

Escalation

Situation Path
Routine coding or workflow question Hardik to Accounting Manager; the AM decides and directs
Client asks about their books, timing, or a transaction AM handles directly (Ricky reviews drafts during her ramp)
Anything touching pricing, scope, or engagement terms Ricky, always. Never negotiate or promise scope solo
Tax or legal advice request Redirect to "your CPA or attorney"
Client frustrated, upset, or gone quiet Flag to Ricky same day with a read on why
Client sent nothing by BD3 Internal escalation same day (protects BD6)
Month materially incomplete at BD6 Escalate BEFORE shipping
Books-level red flags (unusual withdrawals, possible personal spend, missing deposits, negative cash trend) Escalate to Ricky immediately; do not wait for the milestone
Hardik capacity or quality systemically off Escalate to Ricky with specifics: which clients, which patterns

Response standards during close week: acknowledge client email the same business day even if the full answer comes later, answer internal Slack within a couple of working hours, and if a date is at risk the client hears from us before the date.
