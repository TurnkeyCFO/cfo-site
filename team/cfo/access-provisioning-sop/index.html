Purpose

Every role manual tells a new hire to "ask Ricky for access." Nothing tells the granting side what to do. This SOP is the grantor's checklist: for each seat, exactly which systems to provision, at what permission level, and who executes each grant, plus the revocation list for when someone leaves. It closes the class of failure where access is granted ad hoc, credentials are lost mid-onboarding, or a departed teammate keeps access nobody remembered to pull.

Access is provisioned by role, at the least privilege the role needs, through individual identities, never shared logins.

Owner and roles

Person Role in provisioning
Ricky (Founder) Owns the grants system. Executes or authorizes every grant that touches client financial systems, and every admin-level action
Eldine (Brand Manager and VA) Executes delegated, non-financial grants (Slack, calendar, scheduling tools), tracks the onboarding grant checklist, and drives the offboarding revocation list to completion
The new team member Confirms each grant works on receipt and reports any gap the day they find it; never works around a missing login or borrows credentials

Two standing rules that bound everything below: individual identity per person (no shared accounts), and least privilege (grant the narrowest access the seat's work requires, widen only on a real need).

Cadence and triggers

  • On hire, before first work: the new member's role row in the grant matrix is provisioned and each grant is confirmed working. Work does not start on a half-provisioned seat.
  • On role change or tier expansion: re-run the affected rows; widen or narrow access to match the new seat.
  • On departure or engagement end, same day: run the offboarding revocation list to completion.
  • Ongoing: any access gap a team member reports is closed the day it surfaces; any credential that arrives insecurely is re-routed through the secure channel, never accepted by email.

The process

How access is requested and granted

Notes that apply across the matrix:

  • Bill.com and Expensify are read-only for the firm. Where a seat needs them, grant read access only; there is no write or money-movement grant for anyone.
  • Close Command PINs are issued per person. A PIN is casual internal separation, not hard security; it is still issued individually and never shared or posted anywhere a client could reach.
  • The first deliverable of any new seat is the access audit: inventory the stored credentials for each assigned client, test every login that matters for the close, and post any gap the day it is found. This is how a missing or broken grant is caught before it breaks a close.

Offboarding revocation list

When a team member leaves or an engagement ends, run this list to completion the same day. A departure is not done until every applicable row is checked.

The client-offboarding equivalent (pulling the firm's access to a departing client's systems) is covered in the client offboarding checklist; this list is the team-member side.

When something is missing or arrives insecurely

  • A team member who hits a missing or broken login posts the gap the day they find it and never works around it or borrows credentials.
  • A credential that arrives by plain email or chat is not accepted into use; it is re-requested through the secure channel and the sender is told not to send credentials in the clear. This applies to client-provided credentials too.

Guardrails

  • Never provision shared logins. Individual identity per person, always.
  • Never grant more than the seat needs. Least privilege is the default; widen only on a real, stated need.
  • Never grant money-movement rights. The firm does not move client money; Bill.com and Expensify are read-only for everyone.
  • Never accept a credential by plain email or chat. Route it through the secure channel into the vault, or refuse it.
  • Never let a departure sit with access unrevoked. The revocation list runs the same day, to completion.
  • Never post a Close Command PIN, a vault entry, or any credential anywhere a client or an unauthorized person could see it.
  • Never leave a seat half-provisioned. Work starts only when the matrix row is confirmed working.

Escalation

Situation What you do
A new seat needs a client-system grant Ricky executes or authorizes it; Eldine does not grant client-financial access
A credential arrives in plaintext by email Do not use it; re-request through the secure channel and flag it to Ricky as a security event
A team member reports a missing or broken login Close it the day it is reported; if it blocks a close, escalate to Ricky
A departure needs same-day revocation and Ricky is unreachable Eldine runs every non-financial revocation immediately; the client-financial rows (QBO, Ramp, credential rotation) are flagged to Ricky as top priority
Uncertainty about the right permission level for a seat Default to the narrower level and confirm with Ricky before widening
