Purpose

This SOP covers two exits: a client leaving the firm, and a team member or dialer rep leaving the team. Both are access events before they are anything else. Onboarding grants access step by step; offboarding walks the same list in reverse, and a step skipped here is a live credential nobody is watching.

The goal for every client exit: a written end date, one clearly named final close, a complete deliverables package handed over, every access grant revoked, and a farewell that leaves the door open. The goal for every team exit: all access revoked the same day, shared credentials rotated, and every client task reassigned to a named owner before the seat closes.

Owner and roles

Role Who Responsibility in offboarding
Exit decision, final scope, farewell relationship Ricky Sole approver. Confirms the end date, decides the last delivered close, owns the goodbye
Offboarding execution and QC Accounting Manager Runs the checklist, verifies every revocation, confirms the deliverables package is complete
Bookkeeping wrap-up Hardik Completes the final close work assigned through Financial Cents
Admin and comms support Eldine Calendar cleanup, final invoicing coordination, CRM update requests
Systems deprovisioning Ricky (or a delegated ops person) Revokes platform accounts, rotates credentials, rebuilds and republishes the team portal

Cadence and triggers

  • Client trigger: a client gives notice, an engagement ends by its own terms, or Ricky decides to exit the engagement. Nothing in this SOP starts on a verbal hint alone; it starts when the end date is confirmed in writing.
  • Team trigger: a team member or dialer rep resigns, is released, or a contractor engagement ends. The access-revocation lane runs the same day the departure is effective, not at the end of the week.
  • Client timeline: the offboarding checklist should be complete within about two weeks of the final delivered close. Any item still open past that is stalled; surface it to Ricky.
  • Verification pass: the Accounting Manager re-walks the full revocation list after the last item is marked done. Offboarding is finished when the verification pass is clean, not when the last email is sent.

The no-orphaned-owner rule

Every client task, close milestone, and follow-up owned by the departing person is reassigned to a named owner before the seat closes. A close milestone with no owner is a missed close waiting to happen, and a rep's warm callbacks left unowned are booked meetings nobody attends. The seat does not close until the ownership map is whole.

Guardrails

  • No exit runs on a verbal. The client end date is confirmed in writing before any access is removed.
  • The final close meets the full quality bar. Never deliver a weaker last month because the engagement is ending.
  • Revocation is verified, not assumed. Every removal on either lane gets confirmed; asking the client to remove a user is not the same as the user being removed.
  • Nothing is deleted. Client folders archive, they do not disappear. Retention and destruction decisions belong to Ricky (confirm with Ricky).
  • Same-day means same-day. Team access revocation and credential rotation do not wait for a convenient moment.
  • Credentials the person touched get rotated, not just reviewed. That includes the team portal rebuild and republish.
  • No failure language reaches the client. The farewell is warm and clean regardless of why the engagement ended. Anything touching fees, terms, or the reason for the exit is Ricky's to say.
  • Turnkey CFO is a bookkeeping firm. The farewell and handoff language stays in bookkeeping, payroll, 1099, AP/AR, monthly close, and reporting terms. Tax and legal questions still route to the client's CPA or attorney, even on the way out.
  • Client-facing email sends from a role address, never a personal one.
  • Single-writer systems stay single-writer. CRM status changes go through the maintenance process; nobody hand-edits the sheet during an exit.

Escalation

Situation Action
Client end date or final close scope is ambiguous Stop and get Ricky's written confirmation before removing anything
Client asks about fees, refunds, or exit terms Ricky, always. Send a same-day holding acknowledgment and stop there
Client is leaving upset or the exit is relationship-sensitive Flag to Ricky same day with your read; he owns the goodbye
A revocation cannot be completed (client unresponsive, platform will not remove a seat) Surface to Ricky within two weeks of the final close; do not mark the exit complete
A departing team member's access cannot be revoked same-day Escalate to Ricky immediately; treat it as a live security gap, not a to-do
Unsure what the departing person had access to Assume more, not less: inventory from the onboarding access list and rotate anything in doubt
The incoming bookkeeper requests something outside the package Route to Ricky before sharing anything beyond the standard deliverables
