Purpose

This SOP covers how Turnkey CFO runs accounts payable and card spend for clients: intake of payment requests, vendor setup and ACH onboarding, approval chains, card memo and receipt discipline, and the weekly reminder systems that keep uncoded spend from delaying the monthly close. It spans three platforms (Ramp, Bill.com, Expensify) plus the Google Drive intake flow, and it defines exactly what Turnkey can and cannot do on each platform. Read this before touching any client's AP.

The single most important idea in this SOP: Turnkey processes, prepares, and reminds. The default posture, firm-wide, is that Turnkey never initiates or approves money movement; clients approve and money moves only on their authority. Where a client has delegated payment approval to us in an explicit written authorization, that exception is exercised only by Ricky personally, under the Money-movement authority guardrail below.

Cadence and triggers

  • BD1 (business day 1 of the new month): monthly request goes out, including the reminder that card memos are due by BD3.
  • BD3, the memos-by-BD3 rule: all Ramp and Bill.com charge memos for the prior month must be complete. Missing memos are the top cause of a delayed close. A client with nothing submitted by BD3 gets escalated internally the same day.
  • Thursday morning: Expensify cardholder reminders run (per-cardholder emails listing their own uncoded transactions).
  • Friday: Bill.com individualized reminders run (per-person emails listing only what is pending that person's action), and the Friday card-hygiene habit email pattern applies for Ramp cardholders. Each weekly batch is approved by Ricky in Slack before anything sends.
  • On file drop: a client uploading a completed payment-request form to their shared Google Drive Upload folder triggers a notification to accounting@turnkeycfo.com, which starts the bill-pay intake process.
  • Vendor confirmation emails: send in the morning window (roughly 7 to 10am Central) only.

Ramp is the direction of travel: it is the dominant card and AP tool for church clients and is replacing Bill.com and Expensify at several engagements. When a client migrates platforms, update their client dossier and confirm which reminder system covers them (confirm with Ricky).

Ramp card spend and memo discipline

Every card transaction needs three things before it can be coded confidently: a receipt, a memo, and a category. The memo is not optional paperwork, it is the coding signal. At church clients with fund or class tracking, per-cardholder memo context often determines the ministry or fund a charge belongs to, so never default one cardholder's varied spend to a single catch-all account.

Weekly working rhythm:

Automated GL coding via the ramp-classifier runs daily for enrolled clients, with a Slack approval card before anything writes back. It never mutates QBO directly. Confirm which clients are enrolled before relying on it (confirm with Ricky).

Bill.com approval chains and the individualized reminder philosophy

Bill.com clients run their own internal approval chains: a bill routes through one or more approver slots in order, and payment happens only when the chain completes. Turnkey's role is visibility and nudging, never action, because Bill.com is read-only for us.

How we read a chain:

  • A DENIED slot anywhere in the chain is terminal. The bill is dead; nobody gets nagged about it.
  • The current actor is every slot in Approving status (safe for parallel routing), otherwise the first not-yet-approved slot in order.
  • Group slots that nobody has picked up are surfaced in the internal ops summary, never emailed to a person.

The reminder philosophy, and this is a hard rule: reminders are per person, never team-wide. A person receives an email only when something is genuinely pending their action, and the email lists only their items. A named, two-minute personal task gets done; a team broadcast gets ignored. Never add a whole-team blast, and never email someone with nothing pending.

Operating the weekly batch:

Expensify cardholder reminders

For clients still on Expensify, a weekly Thursday-morning run pulls open reports (read-only), flags transactions missing a GL category or a memo, groups them by cardholder, and emails each cardholder a branded list of exactly which of their transactions need attention.

What counts as flagged:

  • Missing category: empty, or parked in an uncategorized holding value.
  • Missing memo: the comment field is empty.
  • Zero-dollar rows with a blank merchant (unposted card holds) are filtered out and never generate a reminder line.

The system discovers client policies dynamically, so a new Expensify client flows in without code changes. It runs behind a preview gate that routes every email to Ricky for review until he explicitly flips it live; confirm the current live status before telling a client their staff are receiving these (confirm with Ricky).

Church AP nuances

Churches carry AP rules that for-profit clients do not. Get these wrong and it is a compliance problem, not just a coding error.

  • Benevolence payments go to vendors only. Pay the utility company or the landlord directly, never the individual being helped. Benevolence requires prior approval and documentation before funds are distributed, filed with the payment record.
  • Fund and class tagging: at fund-accounting churches every transaction needs its fund or class tag at coding time. Distinguish truly restricted funds from ministry-tracking labels; they are not the same thing.
  • Related-party sensitivity: some churches have related-party payments (rent to an affiliated entity, support to a sister congregation) with client instructions about how those appear in reporting. Check the client dossier before touching them.
  • Cash floats: event expenses paid in cash (for example event security) run as a cash-on-hand float with a log, not as slow AP.
  • Tax or legal questions raised by any of the above route to the client's CPA or attorney; we do not advise on them.

Guardrails

  • Default posture: Turnkey never initiates or approves money movement. Bank access is view-only. Bill.com and Expensify are read-only, always, no exceptions. The only exception path is the written, per-client delegation described in Money-movement authority below.
  • Ricky is the sole approver for outbound payments (where a client has delegated that authority in writing) and for every weekly reminder batch. No reminder email reaches client staff without his checkmark or an explicit live-mode decision he made.
  • Never send a team-wide AP blast. Reminders are individualized or they do not go out.
  • Client-facing AP emails send from role addresses (accounting@turnkeycfo.com), carry the client's own finance-team voice where the workflow specifies it, and respect the morning send window for vendor confirmations.
  • No failure language ever reaches a client or vendor. If an automated batch fails or holds, it pages Ricky internally; the recipient sees nothing.
  • Match, do not add: when reconciling AP activity in QBO, match the bank-feed transaction to the existing entry rather than adding a duplicate.
  • Anything touching pricing, scope, or engagement terms found in AP mail routes to Ricky, always.
  • Never work around a missing login or borrow credentials. A platform access gap gets posted to Slack the day it is found.

Money-movement authority

The firm default: Turnkey never initiates a payment and never approves one. We prepare bills, chase memos, and remind approvers; the client's own people release the money. Any exception to that default exists only when all three of the following hold:

  1. The client has given an explicit, per-client, written authorization (an engagement letter clause or a written directive from the client's leadership) delegating payment approval to Turnkey.
  2. The delegated approval is exercised only by Ricky personally. A bookkeeper, accounting manager, or admin never approves or releases a payment, at any client, under any circumstances.
  3. The client appears in the exception list below with the basis of its authorization. A client not listed has no exception; operate under the default posture.

Exception clients and authorization basis:

Client Basis of Ricky's approval authority
Heirloom Church Ricky serves the church personally as its treasurer, a governance seat at the client, alongside the firm engagement. Written authorization on file (confirm with Ricky).
Any other Ramp bill-pay client where Ricky appears as an approver Authorization basis not yet documented here (confirm with Ricky before treating the client as an exception).

The Heirloom arrangement carries a self-review risk: the same person approves the payment as the client's treasurer and runs the firm that books it. The compensating control, required on every cycle:

  • The account manager reviews the monthly AP run after the fact against the approved bill list. Every payment released must trace to an approved request; anything that does not gets flagged to Ricky and documented the same day.
  • The church board and treasurer report includes the disbursement summary, so the board sees every payment made in the period.
  • Between those two reviews, no payment Ricky approves goes unreviewed by a second person.

Apply the same pattern to any future engagement where Ricky holds a governance role at the client: written authorization, Ricky-only execution, an after-the-fact second-person review, and disbursements surfaced in the client's own governance reporting.

Escalation

Situation Action
Large or unusual withdrawal, possible personal charge, missing deposit Escalate to Ricky immediately, do not wait for the weekly rhythm
Client has submitted nothing by BD3 Internal escalation same day (protects the BD6 delivery)
Vendor ACH details not received and payment is time-sensitive Flag to Ricky; do not create the bill early past the hard gate
Benevolence request naming an individual payee Stop; route back to the client for a vendor-directed payment with prior approval
Client asks a tax or legal question in an AP thread Redirect to their CPA or attorney; loop Ricky if the relationship needs handling
Reminder system behaving unexpectedly (wrong recipient, wrong items) Stop the batch, tell Ricky same day
Repeated non-response from a specific cardholder or approver Flag the pattern to Ricky with specifics rather than re-sending forever
