Purpose

This SOP defines how Turnkey communicates: which channel carries which kind of message, how the accounting@ mailbox is triaged, what we have promised clients about the monthly rhythm, how fast we respond, and what our voice sounds like inside the team versus in front of a client. Follow it and every message lands in the right place, in the right tone, from the right address. Turnkey CFO is a bookkeeping firm; our external language is bookkeeping, payroll, 1099, AP/AR, monthly close, and reporting.

Owner and roles

Role Person Communication responsibility
Founder Ricky Named primary point of contact for clients. Sole voice on pricing, scope, and engagement terms. Final reviewer on client-facing memos during team ramp.
Accounting Manager Beth Client-facing liaison as she ramps: answers book, timing, and transaction questions; owns the checkpoint and delivery notes; Ricky reviews her drafts during ramp, then she sends independently.
Bookkeeper Hardik Works inside Financial Cents, not Slack with clients. Compiles open items, reviews mail routed to him in accounting@, flags what needs action.
Brand Manager / VA Eldine Sends the scheduled client touches (monthly request, checkpoint, delivery note, meeting invites and reminders) from role addresses. Does not originate client answers on the books.

Client-facing email always sends from a role-based mailbox (accounting@turnkeycfo.com, Beth@turnkeycfo.com, Ricky@TurnkeyCFO.com), never a personal address.

Cadence and triggers

  • Client emails are acknowledged the same business day, even when the full answer comes later.
  • Internal Slack messages get a reply within a couple of working hours.
  • Open questions for each client are batched into one short note each Friday, with a three business day reply ask to the client.
  • Monthly client touches follow the close calendar: request on the first business day (BD1), checkpoint on the third (BD3), delivery on the sixth (BD6), lock on the tenth (BD10), standing review meetings around BD12.
  • Clients on Ramp receive a short weekly report of card transactions still missing a category or receipt.
  • Weekly per-cardholder "Friday habit" card hygiene emails go to named cardholders, never as a team-wide blast.
  • accounting@ is monitored daily, with extra attention during close week. Thursday is historically the heaviest inbound day, so plan triage time accordingly.

Supporting conventions:

  • Onboarding and prospect follow-up run as one named running thread per contact ("Client / TKCFO - Next Steps", "Client - TK CFO Follow Up") using replies on the same thread, not a new email per touch.
  • System-access requests to a client (Ramp, Bill.com, Gusto, bank view-only) go out as short single-purpose emails, one platform per email, not one long bundle.
  • Statements and documents that arrive by email get filed to the client's Google Drive folder; Financial Cents is not the document of record.
  • Clients are asked to keep ongoing communication in their client portal wherever possible, so requests, documents, and questions live in one place.

Monthly rhythm promises to clients

The client-facing monthly rhythm memo commits us to specific dates. These are promises, not internal targets (confirm with Ricky that the memo distribution is complete before quoting it to a specific client):

  • BD1: the client receives a short, specific list of anything we need (statements, receipts, approvals). If we already have everything, they hear nothing at this step.
  • BD3: one consolidated note of anything still outstanding, plus any transactions we need help labeling. One clear list, not scattered questions through the month.
  • BD6: the client receives their financials as a snapshot, with a short Open Items list showing exactly what is still pending and what it affects.
  • BD10: we finalize, fold in anything new, and close the month. Open items carry forward on the list so nothing is lost.
  • Fridays: open questions arrive as one batched note, with a three business day reply ask.
  • Transaction memos (Ramp, Bill.com, or other card and bill pay tools) are due from the client's team by BD3. Missing memos are the most common reason a close is delayed, and the memo says so plainly.

Two operating implications:

  • The dates hold when the client's information reaches us on time. If a client sends nothing by BD3, escalate internally the same day to protect BD6.
  • If BD6 is at risk, the client hears from Turnkey before the date. We never let a promised date pass silently.

Clients can negotiate cadence details (one client reduced standing meetings to twice a month). Any cadence change is Ricky's call to approve, then it gets noted in the client's dossier.

External voice (client-facing)

  • Warm, professional, plain-spoken, mannerly. Approachable but credible.
  • No emojis, ever. No jargon. No em-dashes.
  • Lead with the answer, then the detail.
  • Frame the client as the hero and Turnkey as the guide: "so your financials always arrive on time," "nothing for you to chase."
  • Reminder copy is collaborative, never naggy: "a couple of minutes," "keeps our books spotless."
  • Never write the phrase that pairs "fractional" with "CFO" in any outward copy. The top service tier is called Reporting & Advisory.
  • Tax and legal questions get redirected to "your CPA or attorney," never answered.
  • Sign-off: current practice is "Warmly, Ricky West" per the live memo template; an older document says "With Gratitude, TurnkeyCFO" (confirm with Ricky which closer is canonical for team members sending under their own names).

Internal voice (team)

  • Direct, peer-to-peer, no filler. Bullets and tables over prose. Lead with the answer.
  • Explain why, not just what.
  • Emojis are fine internally.
  • Default to a Slack channel over a DM so decisions stay findable.

Guardrails

  • Never mix Financial Cents Team Chat and Client Chat. Internal commentary in a client-visible thread is unrecoverable.
  • Never send client-facing email from a personal address. Role mailboxes only.
  • Never answer pricing, scope, or engagement-term questions yourself. That is Ricky, always.
  • Never give tax or legal advice. Redirect to "your CPA or attorney."
  • Never write the banned "fractional" phrase in outward-facing copy.
  • Never let failure language reach a client. If a deliverable or dashboard is broken, hold the send and fix it; a lead pastor or owner never receives a degraded substitute.
  • Never send a team-wide reminder blast when the item concerns one person. Reminders are individualized: one person, only their items.
  • Never work around a missing login or borrow credentials to answer a client faster. Post the access gap to Slack the day you find it.
  • Broad client memos always take the DRAFT for review pass before external send. During ramp, all outgoing client comms from new team members are Ricky-reviewed.
  • Keep each client's own brand on their client-facing assets; never swap in Turnkey's palette on a client deliverable.

Trigger Action

Client frustrated, upset, or gone quiet Flag to Ricky the same day with a short read on why

Month materially incomplete at BD6 Escalate before shipping; never deliver financials you would not stand behind
