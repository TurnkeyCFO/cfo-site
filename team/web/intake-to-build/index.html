Purpose

This SOP covers how a Turnkey Web project moves from a raw lead to a built draft site waiting on approval: where intake happens, how a build request becomes a working preview, what the internal quality bar is, and how the revision loop runs until the draft is ready to show a prospect.

The core idea behind the whole pipeline: we build the site first, then show it. A prospect who can click through a finished draft of their own business converts far better than one reading a proposal. That only works if every draft looks like a bespoke, premium site built for that specific business, which is why the internal review standards below are hard gates, not suggestions.

Nothing in this SOP touches money or client commitments. Pricing, engagement terms, and the send-to-prospect step are Ricky's alone.

Owner and roles

Role Who Responsibility in this flow
Pipeline owner and sole approver Ricky Approves every draft before anything reaches a prospect; owns pricing and scope
Sales reps / SDRs David and the dialer team Generate build requests from calls, review previews for their prospects, send the follow-up email after approval (CC Ricky)
Build engines Automated agents (web-build-agent, turnkey-web-site-builder) Research the business, build the draft, deploy the preview, post the review card to Slack
Admin support Eldine Calendar, client notifications, anything delegatable around the sales motion

The build itself is automated. The humans in the loop are the requester (rep or Ricky) and the approver (Ricky, or a named rep with in-thread sign-off authority). Nobody edits a deployed preview by hand; changes go back through the build pipeline.

Cadence and triggers

A build kicks off from any of these entry points:

  • Intake form (turnkeyweb.org/intakeform): a prospect completes the form; the submission lands in a Google Sheet and posts to the leads Slack channel. The autonomous build agent polls that sheet and starts a build on every new completed inquiry. Fully automatic, no pre-build approval gate; review happens after the build, on the preview link.
  • Instant Estimate and free-website landing submissions: flow into the same leads channel and the same funnel. Church and nonprofit prospects use this exact same path (the industry field tags them); there is no separate church pipeline.
  • BUILD REQUEST card in the Turnkey Web Slack channel: reps or Ricky submit the internal build-request form; a listener picks it up and kicks off a builder session. If the card carries a HubSpot profile link, the builder pulls that record first, because the CRM record the reps maintain beats anything scraped from the web.
  • Direct trigger from Ricky: the phrase "Turnkey web site - (business name or link)" starts a build from nothing more than a name or a URL.

Timing expectations:

  • A build runs start to finish in one automated session (research, build, self-critique, deploy, post). No human touches it mid-build.
  • The first thing a new build system does on go-live is a baseline pass that marks existing backlog as seen and builds nothing. This prevents a day-one stampede. Do not "fix" this.
  • Review the preview card the same business day it posts. A draft sitting unreviewed is a stalled lead.

Step 2: The build

The builder produces one self-contained draft site to a premium bespoke quality bar. Key mechanics the team should understand, even though the agent executes them:

  • Each build is uniquely art-directed for that business. Two builds must never share the same hero treatment, section order, or type pairing. A correct-but-templated build is a fail.
  • The site always covers, in some form: navigation with click-to-call, an immersive hero, real services, social proof, address with map and hours and phone, a closing call to action, a footer, and a mobile sticky call bar.
  • All real business data (phone, address, hours, owner name) is preserved verbatim. If a fact is missing, the section is omitted, never invented.
  • The autonomous intake-form path runs a build, critique, refine loop against a scoring rubric until the draft passes or the iteration cap hits.

Guardrails

  • Preview only, locked. No per-client repos, no turnkeyweb.org subdomains, nothing on a client domain, ever, from this pipeline. The worst case of a fully automatic build must stay "an unreviewed page on a preview URL only we can see."
  • Nothing reaches a prospect without the explicit in-thread approval. No exceptions for "it looked done."
  • Real data only. Missing facts are omitted, never invented. No fabricated reviews, no invented hours, no guessed phone numbers.
  • The prospect's brand, not Turnkey's. No Turnkey purple, logo, or contact info on a draft site.
  • No pricing on draft sites or in build copy. Service pricing conversations happen live with Ricky, never on a page.
  • Bare URLs in Slack. Never wrap a link in asterisks or bold; it breaks click-through.
  • Prospect email addresses stay internal. Never echo a CRM email onto the live site or into a public-facing artifact.
  • Turnkey CFO cross-sell note: if a Web prospect turns out to need bookkeeping, that referral routes to Ricky. Turnkey CFO is a bookkeeping firm; tax or legal questions from any prospect route to "your CPA or attorney."

Escalation

Situation Path
Research finds nothing buildable (no phone, no address, no services) Ask Ricky before building; never fabricate
A draft fails review repeatedly and is not converging Flag to Ricky with a read on why; do not keep iterating blindly
Prospect asks about pricing, scope, or contract terms Ricky, always; reps never quote or promise scope
A preview card sat a full business day with no review Nudge the approver in the thread; a stalled draft is a stalled lead
Build system appears down (no cards posting, requests not picked up) Flag to Ricky; the build agents have status commands and watchdogs he can run
Prospect wants the site live on their domain That is a signed engagement decision, outside this pipeline; route to Ricky

A dedicated Turnkey Web escalation ladder beyond "route it to Ricky" has not been formalized yet (confirm with Ricky). Until it is, when in doubt, the thread plus a direct flag to Ricky is the path.
