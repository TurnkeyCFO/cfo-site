Purpose

This SOP is the exact process for booking discovery meetings on a live Turnkey Web sales call. Booking while the prospect is still on the phone is the single highest-leverage move in the sales motion: a verbal yes plus a calendar invite in their inbox before the call ends converts far better than "I'll send you some times." The whole flow is designed to take under 60 seconds. It covers the shared booking workspace, how to read the color-coded master calendar, the three steps you run on every call, and the guardrails that keep the calendar sane. Written so a brand-new rep can book their first meeting without asking a single question.

Owner and roles

Role Responsibility
Ricky Owns the booking account and the master calendar. Runs the discovery meetings. Blocks the internal master calendar after each Slack ping. Coordinates any early-morning household handoff on his end.
Sales rep Logs into the booking workspace at the start of every dial session, proposes a specific time on the call, creates and sends the invite, and posts the Slack ping immediately after booking.
Eldine Backup for calendar questions and invite cleanup when a rep flags an issue (confirm with Ricky).

Cadence and triggers

  • Runs on every live sales call that reaches a yes: the moment a prospect agrees to a discovery meeting, this flow starts while they are still on the phone.
  • One-time setup per rep: get access to the shared booking account before your first dial session.
  • Per-session setup: log into the booking account at the start of every dial session and keep the Google Calendar tab live and open the entire time you are on the phone.
  • The Slack ping fires the moment the invite is sent, never batched to end of day.

The process

One-time setup: your booking workspace

You log into one shared Google account. Every calendar Ricky uses is already preloaded onto it, so you see real availability across all of life (work, ministry, family) without switching context.

One identity for everything: calendar invites, follow-up emails, and anything client-facing all send from bookings@turnkeyweb.org. Never send client email from your personal Gmail.

Reading the calendar: color = priority

Every event on the master calendar is color-coded. If a prospect's only availability lands on top of an existing event, the color tells you whether you can book over it.

Color What it is Can you book over it?
Orange Heirloom Church ministry blocks Yes. Movable with notice. Easiest to move. Book over it if the client's time is tight.
Green Internal Turnkey work and client check-ins Usually. Second priority. Can typically be shifted if needed.
Red Locked external commitments (day-job blocks) Never. Red cannot be moved. Do not book over red. Find another slot.

Two special cases:

  • Early-morning kids time: if the prospect's only availability is early-morning kids time, go ahead and book it. Send the Slack ping the same way as any other booking. Ricky coordinates the morning handoff on his end.
  • Weekends: Saturday and Sunday are off the table, always. If a prospect only offers weekend availability, push them to the next available weekday.

Guardrails

  • No weekends. Saturday and Sunday are off. Push weekend-only prospects to the next weekday.
  • Never book over red. Red means locked external commitments. Find another slot, no exceptions.
  • Buffer rule: avoid stacking a new meeting inside a 30-minute buffer of an existing event when you can. Prep and transition time matter.
  • Hours rule: the default booking window is 9am to 4pm Central. Early-morning kids time is bookable only when it is the prospect's sole availability, and Ricky coordinates that on his end.
  • One sending identity: everything client-facing (invites, follow-up email) sends from bookings@turnkeyweb.org. Personal Gmail never touches a prospect.
  • Save then Send: an invite the prospect never received does not exist. Always click Send after saving the event.
  • Booked means pinged: a meeting without a same-moment Slack ping in #tk-web is an unfinished booking. The ping is how the master calendar gets blocked.
  • Time zone discipline: quote times in Central and label them CT on the call, in the invite, and in the ping.
  • Booking is not selling scope: if the prospect asks about project terms, pricing, or timelines beyond the meeting itself, note it in the Context line of the ping and leave it for the discovery meeting. Tax or legal questions route to "your CPA or attorney."

Escalation

Situation What to do
Prospect's only availability conflicts with a red block Do not book it. Offer the nearest open weekday slot instead. If the prospect will only take that exact time, book nothing and flag it to Ricky in #tk-web for a decision.
You booked over orange or green Normal and allowed. The Slack ping is the notice; Ricky handles moving the underlying block.
Double-booking or calendar mistake discovered after the invite went out Flag it in #tk-web with @Ricky immediately. Do not silently move or cancel a client-facing invite on your own.
Prospect wants to reschedule after booking Update the event on the bookings calendar, resend the invite, and post an updated BOOKED ping noting the change.
Locked out of the bookings account mid-session Ping Ricky in #tk-web. Do not book from a personal account as a workaround, even once.
Prospect asks something outside booking (pricing, scope, technical) Capture it in the Context line and let the discovery meeting handle it. Tax or legal questions go to "your CPA or attorney."
