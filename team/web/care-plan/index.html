Purpose

This SOP covers the Turnkey Web monthly care plan: what the plan includes, how a client signs up and gets billed, what monthly maintenance means in practice, how edit requests come in and get turned around, how cancellations are handled, and how a care plan client becomes a bigger engagement. The care plan is the recurring revenue engine of Turnkey Web. A build is a one-time project; the care plan is the relationship. Treat every care plan client as a long-term account, not a completed job.

The single most important idea in this SOP: the care plan is a promise of responsiveness. A client pays a flat monthly rate so that when something on their site needs to change, they have one place to send it and it gets done without a new invoice, a new negotiation, or a delay. Protect that promise and the plan retains itself.

Cadence and triggers

  • Sign-up: triggered by a closed sale (a build client rolling onto the plan, or a redesign prospect converting straight onto it). The plan for a build client starts 30 days after go-live, never on the build invoice date.
  • Monthly, automatic: Stripe charges the subscription. The turnkey-web-payments polling reconciler checks payment status on a schedule; there is no webhook, so a failed payment surfaces on the next poll, not instantly.
  • On edit request: whenever a client emails a change. Acknowledge same business day even if the work lands later.
  • Monthly maintenance pass: a light proactive check on each care plan site (confirm the exact checklist and its schedule with Ricky; see the maintenance section below for the working baseline).
  • On cancellation notice: whenever a client asks to stop. Handled within one business day, escalated to Ricky before anything is confirmed back to the client.

The process

What the plan includes

The care plan is a flat monthly subscription. Structural scope (confirm the precise published inclusions list with Ricky before quoting anything in writing):

  • Hosting and deployment. The site lives in the Turnkey Web hub repo and serves through Cloudflare Pages on the brand domain or the client's own domain. The client never manages hosting.
  • Domain and DNS management where Turnkey holds the registration or the Cloudflare zone. Domain registration cost is a small pass-through on top of the plan, agreed at sale.
  • Small content edits: text changes, image swaps, hours and contact updates, adding or removing a testimonial, seasonal banner changes. The kind of change a client describes in one email.
  • Uptime and basic site health. If the site is down or broken, fixing it is our problem, not a billable event.
  • A single point of contact for anything website-related, with a same-business-day acknowledgment.

What the plan does not include: new pages beyond a small count, redesigns, new functionality (booking systems, e-commerce, member portals), SEO campaigns, ad management, and content writing at volume. Those are scoped separately (see upsell paths). The boundary between "an edit" and "a project" is a judgment call; when in doubt, do the small version under the plan and flag the bigger version to Ricky as an upsell conversation, never a surprise invoice.

Sign-up flow: payment link, then welcome page

The standard plan uses one reusable Stripe payment link. It is the same link for every standard-rate client, so there is nothing to generate per client and nothing to approve per client.

If the deal is custom (multiple sites, extra scope, a negotiated rate), do not use the reusable link. Route it through turnkey-web-payments as a per-client subscription. That path posts a Slack approval card and only goes live on Ricky's reaction approval.

Monthly maintenance in practice

Maintenance is mostly quiet. The operational baseline each month per site (confirm the formal checklist with Ricky):

Anything found broken gets fixed as a plan inclusion. Anything found that suggests a bigger opportunity (an outdated section that needs a real redesign, a business that has clearly grown past its site) gets logged as an upsell note for Ricky.

Edit requests: intake, turnaround, delivery

Edit requests arrive by email to the role mailbox the client was given at kickoff (confirm the canonical care plan intake address with Ricky). Clients should never need to chase a personal address or a text thread.

Turnaround norms: small edits within two business days of acknowledgment, same day when trivial (confirm the promised turnaround window with Ricky before publishing it to clients). If a request will take longer, the client hears the new date from us before the old one passes, the same discipline the CFO side uses on deliverable dates.

Content dependencies: if an edit needs something from the client (new photos, approved wording), ask once, clearly, and park the request as waiting-on-client. Nudge after a week. The clock on our turnaround starts when we have what we need, and the client should know that.

Upsell paths from the care plan

Care plan clients are the warmest pipeline Turnkey has. They already pay monthly, already trust the work, and already talk to us. Paths upward:

Path Trigger to watch for What it becomes
Redesign or rebuild Site has outgrown the business, client keeps asking for structural changes A build project on the standard 50/50 split (half upfront, half on completion), with the care plan continuing after the new go-live
New functionality Requests for booking, payments, e-commerce, portals, integrations A scoped one-off project through turnkey-web-payments
More pages or content Repeated "can we add a page for..." requests A scoped content expansion, or a custom retainer above the standard plan
Turnkey CFO cross-referral Client mentions bookkeeping pain, a slow accountant, or messy books Warm intro to the bookkeeping side; this mirrors the dialer rule that flags "already has a website" prospects to Turnkey CFO
Turnkey SEO / Turnkey Design Client asks how to rank, or needs brand work Note the interest for Ricky; SEO is not a live service line yet, so never promise it as available today (confirm current status with Ricky)

The rule on all of these: spot it, log it, hand it to Ricky. Anyone can notice an upsell; only Ricky prices and proposes one.

Guardrails

  • Never quote or write a rate, discount, or refund figure to a client without Ricky's approval. Pricing and scope are Ricky-only, always.
  • Never take a client site down, pause hosting, or suspend service without Ricky's explicit decision.
  • The standard plan uses the reusable payment link; anything custom goes through turnkey-web-payments with its Slack approval card. No hand-built Stripe objects outside that path.
  • All client-facing email sends from a role mailbox, never a personal address. Warm, professional, no emojis, no jargon.
  • Verify every deployed edit on the live URL, desktop and mobile, before telling the client it shipped. Watch for stale cached assets after styling changes.
  • Do not let an edit request quietly become free project work. If it is bigger than an edit, say so warmly and route it to Ricky as scoped work.
  • Tax or legal questions from a client (business structure, sales tax on their products) go to "your CPA or attorney," never answered by us.
  • Care plan sites live in the brand hub repo and deploy through Cloudflare Pages on push. No side deployments, no bare preview links handed to clients.
