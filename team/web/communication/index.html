Purpose

This SOP defines how Turnkey Web talks to prospects and clients: which Slack channels carry which kind of message, how prospect follow-up is paced and approved, what our external copy sounds like, and how a lead moves cleanly from the dialer to Ricky to the build team and back out to the prospect. Follow it and no prospect ever receives an unreviewed message, no warm lead goes stale, and every touch sounds like Turnkey Web: warm, direct, and professional. Websites are the product; credibility is the pitch.

Owner and roles

Role Who Communication responsibility
Founder Ricky Approves every site preview before anything is sent to a prospect. Sole voice on pricing, scope, and engagement terms. CC'd on prospect follow-up emails sent by reps.
Dialing reps The active cold-call reps (see the dialer SOPs) Work the daily call queues, apply the standard disposition tags, post email requests and booking pings to Slack, and send prospect follow-up emails only after an explicit approval.
Build agent Automated build system Builds a draft site from each intake or email request, deploys it to a preview URL, and posts a "ready for review" card with a quality score to Slack. Never contacts a prospect.
Follow-up engine Automated follow-up system Drafts each scheduled follow-up touch and posts a per-touch approval card to Slack. Sends only on an explicit approval reaction; never auto-sends.

Prospect-facing email sends from a role-based mailbox (the shared bookings@ account on the Turnkey Web domain, or a turnkeycfo.com role address where the thread crosses brands), never from anyone's personal email.

Cadence and triggers

  • Intake form and Instant Estimate submissions post automatically to #leads as they arrive. A rep or Ricky reviews new submissions the same business day.
  • Build previews post to #tk-web as soon as the build agent finishes. Ricky reviews and approves or requests changes.
  • Prospect follow-up touches are spaced in business days, not calendar days, so a sequence never lands on a weekend. Touches step outward over time: quick first follow-up, then progressively wider gaps.
  • Each automated follow-up card expires after 48 hours if nobody reacts. An expired card means the touch is skipped, not sent late.
  • A booking ping posts to Slack immediately after a meeting is booked, not batched to end of day.
  • Warm callback queues are worked first every day. Anything still sitting in a warm queue at end of day is a flag, not a carryover.

Notes:

  • Channel names above are the live Slack names. Shorthand like "#tkweb" in older docs means #tk-web (confirm with Ricky if a channel appears to be missing rather than guessing a new one).
  • Approvals happen in the channel, in-thread, so the decision trail stays visible. Never approve a send by DM.
  • Church and nonprofit prospects flow through the same intake funnel and the same channels as for-profit businesses; the industry tag changes, the process does not.

The two approval patterns

Two distinct approval conventions are in use. Both gate a prospect-facing action; they are not interchangeable.

  1. Build approval: a plain-text human reply. When the build agent posts a preview, nothing goes to the prospect until Ricky (or a designated approver) replies in-thread with an explicit written approval naming the company, for example "Acme Auto APPROVED". An emoji reaction is not a build approval.
  2. Send approval: an emoji reaction on a card. Automated follow-up touches post one card per prospect per touch. React with the approve emoji to send, the reject emoji to skip. Cards expire unsent after 48 hours. There is no auto-send path.

Treat these as two tiers: a human sentence approves a new deliverable, a reaction approves a routine scheduled touch (confirm with Ricky whether to formally standardize this split; it is the observed working convention).

Prospect follow-up cadence philosophy

  • One running email thread per prospect. Reply on the same thread for every touch; do not start a new thread per follow-up.
  • Business-day spacing. Sequences are defined in business days so no touch lands on a weekend, and sends go out during business hours in the prospect's workday.
  • Every touch is approval-gated. The first email is not an exception; it gets a card like every other touch.
  • A genuine reply from the prospect stops the sequence. A human takes over the conversation from there.
  • Tone is non-pushy: check in politely, say plainly that we are not rushing any decision, and offer a specific next window ("happy to sync Monday if that works").
  • Expiry is a feature. If nobody approves a touch inside the window, the touch is skipped. We never send stale copy late to catch up.

Handoff: dialer to Ricky to build team to send

This is the chain a cold-dial prospect moves through. Each step has one owner and one visible artifact in Slack.

Handoff hygiene:

  • A prospect who already has a website is disqualified for Web but flagged as a cross-brand referral candidate for Turnkey CFO bookkeeping. Note it; do not discard the contact.
  • If the same business shows up under two reps, flag it for ownership review rather than both reps working it (confirm with Ricky on the standing dedup owner).

Voice rules for external Web copy

These apply to every prospect- and client-facing Web artifact: follow-up emails, site copy, SMS, proposals, and booking invites.

  • Warm, professional, plain-spoken. Lead with the answer, then the detail.
  • No emojis in anything external. No jargon, no filler, no hype.
  • No em-dash characters. Use commas, colons, periods, or parentheses.
  • Write the reader as the hero and Turnkey Web as the guide: their business, their customers, their win; we are the help that gets them there.
  • Never state service pricing, fees, or price tiers in organic content (site pages, blog articles, social posts). Pricing conversations happen live, scoped to the prospect.
  • Turnkey CFO is a bookkeeping firm. In cross-brand referral copy, describe it with bookkeeping language only (bookkeeping, payroll, 1099, AP/AR, monthly close, reporting); never label it a CFO-services firm.
  • Tax or legal questions route to "your CPA or attorney." We do not answer them in copy or on calls.
  • SMS copy always carries the sender identity and a STOP opt-out line, and stays plain ASCII (special characters inflate message encoding and cost deliverability).

Guardrails

  • No message reaches a prospect without its approval step. This has held across every observed prospect chain; keep it unbroken.
  • No personal email accounts for prospect contact, ever. Role addresses only.
  • A hard "not interested" or wrong number is a permanent suppression. That contact is never called, emailed, or texted again by any Turnkey Web sequence.
  • Respect do-not-call and call-recording compliance at all times; reps confirm consent posture through the dialer setup, not ad hoc.
  • No weekend bookings. Default booking window is 9am to 4pm Central; early morning only when it is the prospect's sole availability, booked and flagged internally.
  • Preview links live on the Turnkey Web domain. Never hand a prospect a bare hosting-platform URL.
  • Automated cards that expire stay expired. Do not manually resend an expired touch without rereading it for freshness first.
  • Do not edit disposition tag names, follow-up intervals, or the campaign structure to "improve" comms pacing. Those are locked; changes go through Ricky.

Escalation

Situation Path
Prospect asks about pricing, scope, or terms Ricky, always. Reps never quote or negotiate.
Prospect is frustrated, confused, or goes quiet after showing interest Flag to Ricky in #tk-web the same day with a short read on why.
Build preview sits unapproved and the prospect is waiting Nudge in-thread in #tk-web; if still unapproved by the next business day, tag Ricky directly.
Follow-up card about to expire on a genuinely warm prospect Ping Ricky before the 48-hour window closes rather than letting a hot lead skip a touch.
Warm callback queue not empty at end of day Raise it in #tk-web that evening; warm leads are the highest-intent contacts we have.
Same prospect claimed by two reps Pause outreach from both, flag for ownership review (confirm with Ricky).
Tax or legal question from a prospect "Your CPA or attorney." Do not improvise an answer.
