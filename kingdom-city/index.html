The Story in Four Movements

What the numbers say
1 · The funding flip. KCC launched on outside money (sending church + partners) and has crossed into congregation-funded. That is the single most important trend in the whole dataset — the difference between a subsidized plant and a self-sustaining church. Protect it: keep growing the giver base.
2 · Razor-thin margin. “Break-even” is +$825/mo, not a cushion. One soft giving month or one staff add tips it negative. The balance sheet ($128k reserve, no debt) is what absorbs the wobble — treat it as the safety net it is, not spendable surplus.
3 · The buildout was a one-time hit. The FY26 deficit was driven by the portable-church investment and the worship-leader hire, not a broken run-rate. Strip those out and the operating picture is near break-even. Don’t budget FY27 off FY26’s spike.
4 · Bookkeeping hygiene unlocks clarity. ~$45k of recent congregational giving still sits un-split in the parent QBO 40000 account, and the 40001 Tithes line stopped posting. Categorizing it is the fastest way to make QBO match the Tithely reality — a near-term Turnkey CFO cleanup item.

Forecast Sensitivity — Read Before Trusting the Plan

Assumption risk
The expense base is the swing factor. The future deficit or surplus is decided almost entirely by one number: how much the church spends per month. The Long-Range Plan’s seeded “starting monthly expense” figures ran materially above the trailing-24-month average. We’ve set the Forecast Builder’s default to the disciplined case (~$26.9k/mo, ~$322k/yr). If KCC instead scales spend to ~$82k/mo, FY27 swings to a ~$700k deficit — not because giving collapses, but because the cost base outruns it. Use the slider to pressure-test both.
The revenue engine is simpler and more reliable. Revenue is mostly attendance × giving-per-attender. At $160/mo per attender, every +25 in average attendance is roughly +$48,000/yr in giving. Growth of the giver base — not the gift size — is the durable lever; KCC has already proven it can move that number (20 → 52 active givers).
