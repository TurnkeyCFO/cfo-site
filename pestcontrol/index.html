Deep working knowledge of pest control finances.

Recurring contract MRR

Contract count doesn't tell you if you're growing or just replacing churn.

Monthly recurring revenue tracks the actual value of your subscription base — net of cancellations, upgrades, and new adds. Knowing your MRR, churn rate, and average contract value lets you make real decisions about marketing spend, route expansion, and acquisition vs retention investment.

Chemical COGS per stop

Not all service types have the same chemical cost profile.

General pest service uses minimal chemical per stop. Termite treatments, mosquito programs, and fumigations are chemical-intensive. Without tracking chemical cost per service type, you can't tell which services are priced correctly — or which are eroding your gross margin as input costs rise.

Route profitability

Route density determines your actual cost per stop.

Drive time between stops is a fixed labor cost. Routes with low geographic density have higher cost per stop than dense urban routes at the same contract price. Without route-level cost tracking, you can't identify which routes to reprice, consolidate, or walk away from at renewal.

Deferred revenue on prepaid contracts

Annual prepaid contracts aren't income at collection.

When a customer prepays an annual contract, you've collected cash but not yet earned the revenue. Booking it all at collection overstates income in Q1 and understates it for the rest of the year. We recognize prepaid contract revenue monthly as service is performed — correct from day one.

Workers' comp by license level

Licensed applicators and technicians carry different rates.

Licensed structural pest control applicators and general field techs carry different workers' comp classification codes. Coding everyone under one class overpays premiums and creates audit exposure at renewal. Correct classification set up from first payroll, reconciled annually.

Vehicle & equipment fleet

Service vans and spray equipment are fixed assets.

Service vehicles, spray rigs, and major equipment are fixed assets depreciated over 5–7 years. Section 179 and bonus depreciation can accelerate deductions in the purchase year. We track every asset from purchase and reconcile to your CPA's return categories at year-end.
