Executive Summary

The short version — before the detail.

New Hope Maui is a ministry of roughly $500,000 in annual giving, operating in one of the highest cost-of-living communities in the country. This proposal lays out three ways to build the financial foundation that protects that giving. It recommends the full six-month build — though every option leaves the church stronger and better-stewarded than it found it.

The build is not an expense the church carries indefinitely. It is a one-time foundation — paid for over six months, then owned and run by New Hope's own team at a modest $500/month. And on conservative assumptions, it is anticipated to return more than it costs inside the first year.

Our Commitment to New Hope

New Hope does not have to take the return on faith.

The six-month build is the only fixed term — after it, every month is month-to-month, with no long-term contract. And if, twelve months after the build is complete, the engagement has not produced documented financial value at least equal to its cost, we keep advising New Hope at no additional charge until it has.

Financial infrastructure is stewardship, not overhead. A church that builds it well protects the generosity of everyone who gives.

What ~$500K in annual giving represents

Roughly 100–150 committed adults giving $3K–$5K each year — most of them dual-income families carrying a real cost of living.

A 1% improvement in how that giving is gathered and stewarded is harder-won here than on the mainland — which is exactly why the system that produces it is worth building properly.

Why it matters for New Hope

Audit-ready at all times

Every account reconciled monthly — no year-end scramble, no costly reconstruction, nothing the board has to apologize for.

Hours returned to ministry

Removes roughly 4 hours a month of financial admin from staff and leadership.

The lowest-cost form of protection

At roughly 1.2% of annual revenue, clean books are the most affordable safeguard a church its size can put in place.

After the Build

The engagement ends. The system stays. The church owns it.

The most common — and most reasonable — concern about outside financial help is the fear of permanent dependence. This build is designed specifically to remove that fear.

→

Bookkeeping continues at $500/mo

No surprise increases. No long-term contract. A predictable, modest line item the board can plan around for years.

→

Every system, report, and process remains in place

The build is engineered to keep running without us — it belongs to New Hope.

→

Staff is trained to operate it internally

The goal is sustainability, not dependency. The church's own people run the day-to-day.

→

Optional advisory continues only if it's genuinely helpful

Beyond the build, we earn the right to stay each month — never assume it.

This is a one-time foundation, not a permanent expense. New Hope pays to build the system once — and then owns it. That is the difference between a cost and an investment.
