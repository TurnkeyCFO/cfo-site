Tip credit payroll complexity

FLSA tip credit isn't optional — it's a federal compliance requirement.

The $2.13 federal tip credit rate requires that declared tips always bring an employee to at least $7.25/hr (or your state minimum). Tip pooling arrangements must be documented and compliant. We track declared tips, verify the wage floor every pay period, and maintain the documentation trail your labor attorney would want to see.

Texas mixed-beverage tax

A separate 6.7% gross receipts tax on top of sales tax — for every mixed beverage permit holder.

Texas TABC permit holders pay the mixed-beverage gross receipts tax on all alcohol sales in addition to regular sales tax. Food sales, non-alcoholic beverage sales, and alcohol sales must each be tracked separately. We set up the chart of accounts to separate them from day one so your tax remittance is right every month.

Delivery app commission netting

The net deposit is not revenue — it's revenue minus what the platform took.

DoorDash, Uber Eats, and Grubhub deduct their commissions (15–30% of order value) before remitting to you. Recording only the deposit understates true revenue and hides the true cost of third-party delivery. We record each platform's gross sales as revenue and its commissions as cost of sales so you see the real margin on delivery orders.

Gift card deferred revenue

Gift cards sold are a liability until redeemed.

When a guest buys a $100 gift card, you haven't earned $100 — you've taken on a $100 obligation. Revenue is recognized only when the card is redeemed at the register. We track your gift card liability balance monthly and recognize income at redemption, so your P&L reflects meals actually served, not cash collected for future meals.

Prime cost ratio management

The most important number in restaurant finance — COGS plus labor as % of revenue.

Profitable independent restaurants typically run prime cost between 55% and 65% of revenue. Drift above 65% means food cost, labor, or both are out of control. We track prime cost monthly with enough detail to know whether the problem is protein cost, over-scheduling, or delivery platform erosion — so you can fix the right thing.

Seasonal & catering revenue spikes

Holiday rushes and private events need separate tracking to be useful.

Catering events, buyouts, and holiday revenue spikes distort your regular operating metrics if lumped together. We track catering revenue and direct costs separately so your per-week restaurant baseline stays clean, and your catering margin is visible on its own. Useful when you're deciding whether to grow that business line — or price it more aggressively.

Questions restaurant owners ask us first

Do you replace our CPA?

No — we work alongside your CPA. We keep books clean and filing-ready year-round so your CPA can file without a month of cleanup. If you need a CPA who understands food service, we can refer one.

Can you handle tip payroll for servers and bartenders?

Yes. We handle tip credit payroll under FLSA — tracking declared tips, verifying servers always clear minimum wage, documenting tip pooling arrangements, and reconciling tip income to POS reports monthly.

How do you handle Texas mixed-beverage sales tax?

Texas imposes a 6.7% mixed-beverage gross receipts tax on top of regular sales tax for alcohol sales. We separate food, non-alcoholic beverage, and alcohol sales in your chart of accounts and prepare the data for correct remittance every month.

Can you reconcile DoorDash, Uber Eats, and Grubhub payouts?

Yes. Delivery platforms pay net — after commissions, service fees, and promotions are deducted. We record gross revenue, then separately record platform commission expense so your P&L shows true revenue and true cost of delivery sales, not just what hit your bank account.

What is prime cost and why does it matter?

Prime cost is your total food and beverage COGS plus total labor cost as a percentage of revenue. Most profitable independent restaurants run between 55–65%. We track it monthly so you can spot drift before it becomes a crisis.

How do you handle gift card revenue?

Gift cards sold are a liability, not revenue — revenue is recognized only when the card is redeemed. We record gift card sales as deferred revenue and reduce the liability at redemption, so your P&L reflects actual meals served, not cash collected for future meals.

Is our data secure?

Everything lives in QuickBooks Online, Gusto, and Ramp — enterprise-grade platforms with role-scoped access. Credentials are never shared by email and we maintain professional liability insurance.
