Bill pay moves to Ramp

Your accounts payable moves onto Ramp, which removes the monthly Bill.com fee, tightens approvals, and feeds straight into your books.

Today your bills run through Bill.com at a monthly subscription cost. We replace that with Ramp, which is free to you, handles bill pay and card controls in one place, and syncs directly into QuickBooks. We migrate your vendors and recurring payments over so nothing is missed, then wind Bill.com down at month-end. Your approval still gates every payment, so you stay in control of what goes out and when.

Your transition timeline

Two tracks running in parallel, the books and bill pay, with clear line of sight on each. Our promise: clean and fast, with a real cleanup of the back history.

No coverage gap during the handoff: please have your current provider close out June as they normally would. We'll slightly overlap with them through the first week or two of July as we take the handoff, and that overlap is intentional so nothing slips through the cracks. Your first close fully on us is July's books, delivered in early August.
Books, Reporting & Advisory Phase
  • First (this week): We set up your client portal and deliver your engagement letter and payment request. You e-sign and set up payment, then we begin.
  • Week 1 (Jun 24–30): Accept access (QuickBooks, bank view-only, giving platform, payroll). Verify the books and bank feeds. Schedule your onboarding call.
  • Books transition (July 1): We take the books. Cleanup begins on the back history, the chart of accounts rebuild, and standing up proper fund accounting.
  • First 90 days (Advisory Phase): Full cleanup, fund-accounting stand-up, annual budget build, cash-flow forecast, restricted-fund and giving reporting, and your live dashboard plus board package.
  • First close (early August, for July): Your first full monthly close, live dashboard, and a board-ready summary, delivered ahead of your board meeting.
Bill Pay Transition — Ramp live in July
  • This week: You approve the Ramp application and send entity details (Section 3).
  • This week: Connect and verify the bank account that funds bill payments.
  • Early July: We build Ramp — load vendors, recreate recurring bills, set approval routing and card controls.
  • Mid-July: Migrate active vendors off Bill.com; first bills paid through Ramp for your approval.
  • End of July: Bill.com winds down, the monthly fee stops, and AP runs on Ramp.
  • Ongoing: We enter and schedule bills in Ramp for your approval, with no separate subscription to manage.
