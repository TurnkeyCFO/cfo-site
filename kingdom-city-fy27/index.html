The headline

$271,006 in, $247,219 out — a $23,787 operating surplus.

FY27 is a healthy year on paper: revenue of $271,006 against operating costs of $247,219 leaves a $23,787 surplus — a 9% margin — plus a separate $39,710 Vision Fund. The encouraging engine underneath: attendance rising to 162 and internal giving climbing all year. The caution: most of this budget is still funded from outside the room.

Where the money comes from

Only 30 cents of every dollar is given by the people in the room.

Of $271,006 in operating income, $82,306 comes from internal tithes — about 30%. The other $188,700 (70%) is external and partner support. The trend is the right one: internal giving rises every month as attendance grows, while outside support gently declines.

The monthly trajectory — internal giving rising as outside support fades

The trend is the good news. Internal tithes grow from $6,300 to $7,424/mo across the year as attendance climbs to 162. The strategic job: keep that line rising fast enough to replace external dollars as they sunset.
The cliff ahead

FY27 is the peak. On the current plan, the surplus turns to a $24,674 deficit within two years.

This is the most important picture in the budget. External and partner support is scheduled to fall $82,556 over the next two years as church-plant partners graduate Kingdom City off support. Internal giving grows $30,539 in the same window — real progress, but not enough to cover the drop. The result: the Model itself shows net income sliding from $23,787 (FY27) to $-12,041 (FY28) to $-24,674 (FY29).

Net operating income, FY26 → FY29

The squeeze: outside support vs internal giving

Read this as a countdown, not a crisis. FY27's surplus is exactly the runway the church needs — two years to grow internal generosity enough to stand on its own before outside support thins out. The budget's job is to make that countdown visible so the leadership uses the good year to prepare for the lean ones.

The work ahead

Use the surplus year to outrun the sunset.

FY27 buys time. Three levers turn the coming $24,674 FY29 shortfall back into balance — and the first two are already in motion as the church grows.

Lever 1 · Generosity
+$10/attendee/mo

Lifting average giving by about $10/attendee/month by FY29 closes the projected shortfall through internal giving alone — discipleship in giving, not a new revenue source.

Lever 2 · Growth
151→199by FY29

The Model already assumes attendance grows to 199. Every person above that — and every new giver among them — compounds internal giving against the sunset.

Lever 3 · Retention
Holdthe partners

Every partner dollar retained past its scheduled sunset is a dollar internal giving doesn't have to replace. Partner stewardship buys more runway for levers 1 & 2.

The bottom line for FY27. Kingdom City is doing the right things — growing attendance, growing giving, finishing the year in surplus. The budget simply asks leadership to treat this surplus as a two-year runway: keep the internal-giving line climbing, steward the partner relationships, and the church arrives at self-sustaining before the outside support thins out.
