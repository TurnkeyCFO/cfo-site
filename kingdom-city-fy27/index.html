Where the money comes from

Only 30 cents of every dollar is given by the people in the room.

The opportunity

The FY27 mission: grow the share the church gives itself.

FY27 finishes in surplus — but 70% of it still comes from outside the room. The healthiest version of this budget shifts that mix toward internal giving. Three levers move it, and the first two are already in motion as the church grows.

Lever 1 · Generosity
$45/attendee/mo

FY27 internal giving runs about $45 per attendee per month. Every dollar that rises here replaces an external dollar and strengthens the foundation.

Lever 2 · Growth
140→162this year

Attendance grows from 140 to 162 across FY27. Each new person — and each new giver among them — compounds internal giving toward self-sufficiency.

Lever 3 · Stewardship
70%external today

External and partner support funds 70% of FY27. Stewarding those relationships well — while internal giving climbs — is how the church earns its independence.

The bottom line for FY27. Kingdom City is doing the right things — growing attendance, growing giving, finishing the year $23,787 in surplus. The single goal that makes every future year easier: keep raising the share of the budget the church funds itself.
