The dependency

70% of the budget rests on money the church doesn't yet generate itself.

External and partnership support is a gift and a clock. It funds the plant today, but it is scheduled to decline ~20% a year as church-plant partners graduate Kingdom City off support. Two named partners — Shamblee Church and Georgia State FCA — are slated to sunset by June 2027.

Seasonality & cash

Income is steady; spending is lumpy. The gap compounds as the year runs.

Operating income arrives in a fairly even line each month, but spending spikes around fall launch (Sep), year-end and spring events. Because monthly costs sit above monthly income for most of the year, the cumulative operating shortfall deepens — reaching $51,510 by June. This is the cash strain leadership should fund deliberately, not absorb by surprise.

Monthly income vs expense, with cumulative operating net

Plan the runway. The heaviest months are September (fall kickoff + outreach) and the spring event stretch. A modest operating reserve — or partner timing that front-loads support — keeps these spikes from becoming cash crises.

Closing the gap

Three levers close a $51.5K gap — and two of them are already moving.

The operating gap is real but not large in absolute terms. Here is the honest math on what it takes to erase it, and why the trend is on the church's side if internal giving keeps climbing.

Lever 1 · Generosity
+$28/attendee/mo

Lift average giving from ~$45 to ~$73 per attendee per month and internal tithes alone close the gap — no other change needed.

Lever 2 · Growth
+95attendees

At today's ~$45/mo, roughly 95 more people in the room (avg 151 → 246) covers it through giving as attendance compounds.

Lever 3 · Retention
Holdthe partners

Every partner dollar retained past its sunset is a dollar internal giving doesn't have to replace this year. Partner stewardship buys time for levers 1 & 2.

The bottom line for FY27. Kingdom City is a healthy plant doing the right thing — growing its own giving base while it still has outside support. The budget simply asks leadership to name the $51,510 gap, fund the seasonal cash spikes, and keep the internal-giving line climbing. Do that, and FY28 is where the church's own generosity carries the mission.
