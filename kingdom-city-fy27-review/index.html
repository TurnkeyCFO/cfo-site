02 — How the church is funded

More than half of FY27 income still comes from outside.

Kingdom City is funded by member giving, a schedule of partner churches, and the Vision Fund's designated giving. The plan deliberately lets that outside support fall away as member giving climbs — so each year the church stands more firmly on its own. Today 54% of income is external; by FY29 that is 21%.

03 — The giving engine

Two assumptions drive the entire plan.

Everything on this page rests on two levers: how many people gather, and how much each one gives. Attendance rises from 140 to 162 across FY27, and giving per attendee nearly doubles from $45 to $86.25 a month — pushing toward Blake's $90 target by July 2027 and $160 by June 2029. Move either lever and every number moves with it.

05 — People & payroll

The team behind the budget.

Personnel is the largest line in any church budget, and at 37.3% of income Kingdom City sits comfortably below the healthy 45–55% band — further under than most churches at this stage. Four interns raise their own support, so the church carries no net personnel cost for them at all.

07 — The month by month

The year does not fund itself evenly.

Income rises steadily every month while expenses spike in the fall. September is the one deep-dip month — the fall ministry and outreach season lands all at once — with October roughly breakeven and December slightly negative. The other nine months carry the year comfortably to a full-year surplus.

08 — What the plan produces

FY27 is the investment year. FY28 and FY29 are the payoff.

As tithes triple and partner support sunsets, the surplus grows from $23,217 to $146,709 over three years — every year positive. FY27 carries the heaviest launch cost and still finishes ahead; the plan strengthens from here.

The honest read on FY27

On operating giving alone, FY27 runs a modest deficit: $308,685 in against $325,178 out is −$16,493. The Vision Fund's $39,710 in designated giving is what carries the year to a +$23,217 surplus. That is the right frame: FY27 is an investment year, funded partly by designated vision giving, on the way to an operating surplus in FY28.

The three headlines

What FY27 sets in motion.

  1. Tithes triple. Internal giving grows from $119,985 in FY27 to $342,977 by FY29 as attendance and per-attendee giving both climb.
