02 — How the church is funded

More than half of FY27 income still comes from outside.

Kingdom City is funded by member giving, a schedule of partner churches, and the Vision Fund's designated giving. The plan deliberately lets that outside support fall away as member giving climbs — so each year the church stands more firmly on its own. Today 54% of income is external; by FY29 that is 21%.

03 — The giving engine

Two assumptions drive the entire plan.

Everything on this page rests on two levers: how many people gather, and how much each one gives. Attendance rises from 140 to 162 across FY27, and giving per attendee nearly doubles from $45 to $86.25 a month — pushing toward Blake's $90 target by July 2027 and $160 by June 2029. Move either lever and every number moves with it.

05 — People & payroll

The team behind the budget.

Personnel is the largest line in any church budget, and at 42.2% of income Kingdom City sits just under the healthy 45–55% band. Four interns raise their own support, so the church carries only a short $12,000 bridge before that cost disappears entirely.

The intern program

Four support-raised interns at $1,500/month launch September 1, 2026. They raise their own support, so the church only fronts the first paychecks: $6,000 in September, $4,000 in October, $2,000 in November, then $0 from December on. A $60,000 gross program costs the church a net $12,000 in FY27, and nothing thereafter.

07 — The month by month

The year does not fund itself evenly.

Income rises steadily every month while expenses spike in the fall. September and October run negative — the intern program launches before its support ramps, and the fall event season lands — but the other ten months carry the year comfortably to a full-year surplus.

08 — What the plan produces

FY27 is the investment year. FY28 and FY29 are the payoff.

As tithes triple and partner support sunsets, the surplus grows from $10,586 to $111,602 over three years — every year positive. FY27 carries the heaviest launch cost and still finishes ahead; the plan strengthens from here.

The honest read on FY27

On operating giving alone, FY27 runs a deficit: $308,685 in against $337,809 out is −$29,124. The Vision Fund's $39,710 in designated giving is what carries the year to a +$10,586 surplus. The three headlines

What FY27 sets in motion.

  1. Tithes triple. Internal giving grows from $119,985 in FY27 to $342,977 by FY29 as attendance and per-attendee giving both climb.
  2. External support sunsets. Outside dependency falls from 54% to 34% to 21% as partner churches taper on schedule.
  3. Every year is positive. The surplus climbs from $10,586 to $66,661 to $111,602 — FY27 is the low point of the plan, and it is still in the black.
