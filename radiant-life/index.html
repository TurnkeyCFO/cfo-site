What I Heard

Here is what I took away from our conversation

RW
Ricky WestTurnkey CFO · after our call this morning

Phillip, thank you for the time this morning. I want to play back what I heard, because getting this right matters to you and it matters to me.

The clock is the headline. Your current bookkeeper is leaving by July 15, and the one thing that absolutely cannot slip is payroll. A pastor and staff counting on a paycheck is not a place for a gap. So before anything else, that date is the promise I am making: payroll runs on time, no scramble, no missed beat.

You have carried the treasurer role since 2010 — fifteen years of Sunday counts, reconciliations, and quarterly filings. You are ready to hand it off and, in time, to step out of the seat entirely. My job is to make that handoff feel like relief, not risk.

The picture is more layered than a typical church. You own a strip center and three offices managed by an outside property manager, so there are rental statements to reconcile and analyze every month. You track a building fund and restricted dollars like the camp fundraiser, and those can never blur into general operating. And the giving side runs through Planning Center, which is a big part of the financial story for a church your size.

You also mentioned the budget. Right now the six-month budget process lives on your shoulders. Over time, I would love to take the whole thing — every department, every fund — so leadership walks into the year with a plan, not a spreadsheet scramble.

And for what it is worth, this is familiar ground. I already do exactly this work for Heirloom Church — restricted funds, giving analytics, pastor-facing reporting and all. You would not be my first church. You would be in good company.
